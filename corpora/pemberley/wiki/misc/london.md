London appears in *Pride and Prejudice* as a divided social geography.
[Mr. and Mrs. Gardiner](../persons/mr-gardiner.md) — [Mrs. Bennet](../persons/mrs-bennet.md)'s
brother and sister-in-law — live in Gracechurch Street, near Cheapside, "within view of" Mr.
Gardiner's own warehouses.[^s1] [Caroline Bingley](../persons/caroline-bingley.md) and
[Mrs. Hurst](../persons/louisa-hurst.md) disparage the Bennets' "low connections" for having an
uncle who is an attorney in [Meryton](meryton.md) and another who lives "somewhere near
Cheapside."[^s2] [Elizabeth Bennet](../persons/elizabeth-bennet.md) later remarks sardonically that [Mr. Darcy](../persons/fitzwilliam-darcy.md)
would think "a month's ablution" not enough to cleanse him of having entered Gracechurch
Street.[^s3]

By contrast, when the [Bingley](../persons/charles-bingley.md) party removes to London, they
dine in Grosvenor Street, where [Mr. Hurst](../persons/mr-hurst.md) has a house; it is there
that [Jane Bennet](../persons/jane-bennet.md), visiting her aunt and uncle Gardiner, calls on
Caroline Bingley.[^s4]

When [Lydia Bennet](../persons/lydia-bennet.md) elopes with
[George Wickham](../persons/george-wickham.md), the couple are traced only as far as London before
their trail is lost; [Mr. Bennet](../persons/mr-bennet.md) and then
[Mr. Gardiner](../persons/mr-gardiner.md) search the city for them, and Mr. Gardiner eventually
finds them there and negotiates their marriage from Gracechurch Street, where Lydia stays until the
wedding.[^s5]

## Lady Catherine calls on Darcy

On her way back from confronting Elizabeth at Longbourn, Lady Catherine stops in London and calls on
[Darcy](../persons/fitzwilliam-darcy.md), repeating Elizabeth's defiant answers in hopes of turning
him against her — an account that instead gives him hope of her real feelings.[^s6]
