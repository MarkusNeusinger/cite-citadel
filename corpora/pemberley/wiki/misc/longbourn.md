Longbourn is the village and estate where [Mr.](../persons/mr-bennet.md) and
[Mrs. Bennet](../persons/mrs-bennet.md) and their five daughters live, one mile from
[Meryton](meryton.md); the Bennets are "the principal inhabitants" of the village.[^s1] The estate is worth about two thousand a year and is entailed,
in default of male heirs, on [William Collins](../persons/william-collins.md), Mr. Bennet's
cousin.[^s2] Collins is to inherit Longbourn after Mr. Bennet's death, a prospect that is a
running source of anxiety and resentment for Mrs. Bennet.[^s3]

Longbourn is thrown into crisis when [Lydia Bennet](../persons/lydia-bennet.md) elopes with
[George Wickham](../persons/george-wickham.md) from Brighton: [Mr. Bennet](../persons/mr-bennet.md)
leaves for London to search for her, [Mrs. Bennet](../persons/mrs-bennet.md) takes to her room, and
[Jane](../persons/jane-bennet.md) and [Elizabeth](../persons/elizabeth-bennet.md) manage the
household until their uncle [Mr. Gardiner](../persons/mr-gardiner.md) secures a marriage
settlement.[^s4] After the wedding, Mr. Bennet at first refuses to receive the couple at Longbourn,
but relents to a single visit before they join Wickham's new regiment in the north.[^s5]

## Lady Catherine's visit and two engagements

[Lady Catherine de Bourgh](../persons/lady-catherine-de-bourgh.md) arrives unannounced at Longbourn
to confront [Elizabeth Bennet](../persons/elizabeth-bennet.md) over a rumoured engagement to
[Darcy](../persons/fitzwilliam-darcy.md), demanding — and failing to obtain — a promise that she
will never accept him.[^s6] Within the same weeks,
[Bingley](../persons/charles-bingley.md) proposes to [Jane](../persons/jane-bennet.md) at Longbourn,
and Darcy proposes again to Elizabeth on a walk near the house; both sisters become engaged there
before the household disperses to their respective married homes.[^s7][^s8]
