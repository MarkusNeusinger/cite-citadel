Hunsford is a parish near Westerham, in Kent, where
[William Collins](../persons/william-collins.md) holds the rectory under the patronage of
[Lady Catherine de Bourgh](../persons/lady-catherine-de-bourgh.md).[^s1] The parsonage garden is
separated from [Rosings Park](rosings-park.md), Lady Catherine's residence, by only a lane.[^s2]
After marrying Collins, [Charlotte Lucas](../persons/charlotte-lucas.md) moves to Hunsford and
invites [Elizabeth Bennet](../persons/elizabeth-bennet.md) to visit her there; her early letters
describe the house, neighbourhood, and roads as all to her taste.[^s3]

[Elizabeth Bennet](../persons/elizabeth-bennet.md) visits Hunsford for six weeks in March and
April, travelling with [Sir William Lucas](../persons/sir-william-lucas.md) and his daughter
[Maria](../persons/maria-lucas.md); it is there that
[Mr. Darcy](../persons/fitzwilliam-darcy.md) proposes to her and, refused, gives her the long
letter that overturns her opinion of him and of [Wickham](../persons/george-wickham.md).[^s4] The
parsonage itself is "rather small, but well built and convenient," its garden tended personally by
Collins, who spends much of his time there or in his book-room while the ladies sit in a
back-facing drawing-room.[^s5]
