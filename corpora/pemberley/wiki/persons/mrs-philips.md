Mrs. Philips is [Mrs. Bennet](mrs-bennet.md)'s sister, married to [Mr. Philips](mr-philips.md),
and lives in [Meryton](../misc/meryton.md), where she is a favourite source of local news for her
nieces [Kitty](kitty-bennet.md) and [Lydia Bennet](lydia-bennet.md).[^s1] She is "always glad to
see her nieces," and on meeting [George Wickham](george-wickham.md) and Mr. Denny in the street
she throws up her parlour window to press an invitation on them.[^s1]

She hosts a card and lottery-ticket party attended by [William Collins](william-collins.md),
Wickham, and several officers; Collins's elaborate compliments leave her "quite awed," and her
opinion of his consequence rises further once she learns the scale of
[Lady Catherine de Bourgh](lady-catherine-de-bourgh.md)'s [Rosings](../misc/rosings-park.md)
estate, which he describes to her at length.[^s2]

During [Lydia](lydia-bennet.md)'s elopement crisis, Mrs. Philips comes to Longbourn and stays
several days to support [Jane](jane-bennet.md) while their father is away in London, and is
afterward a source of fresh gossip about [Wickham](george-wickham.md)'s debts and misconduct
around Meryton.[^s3]

## Tax on Darcy's forbearance

After Elizabeth's engagement to [Fitzwilliam Darcy](fitzwilliam-darcy.md) becomes known, Mrs.
Philips's vulgarity is "another, and, perhaps, a greater tax" on his patience than Sir William
Lucas's obsequiousness; she stands in too much awe of Darcy to speak to him with familiarity, but
whenever she does speak, "she must be vulgar."[^s4]
