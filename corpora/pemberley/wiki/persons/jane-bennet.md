Jane Bennet is the eldest of the five Bennet daughters and is widely considered the great beauty
of the family.[^s1] At the [Meryton](../misc/meryton.md) assembly she is judged "the only handsome girl in the room" by
[Mr. Darcy](fitzwilliam-darcy.md) and dances twice with [Mr. Bingley](charles-bingley.md), more
than with any other partner.[^s1] Her sister [Elizabeth](elizabeth-bennet.md) observes that Jane
"united...great strength of feeling" with "a composure of temper and an uniform cheerfulness of
manner," which keeps the depth of her growing regard for Bingley hidden from the world.[^s2]

## Illness at Netherfield

Sent to dine at [Netherfield](../misc/netherfield-park.md) on horseback at her mother's
contrivance, Jane is caught in the rain and falls seriously ill, remaining at Netherfield for
several days while Elizabeth nurses her.[^s3]

## Bingley's departure

After the Netherfield ball, the Bingley party quits Netherfield for [London](../misc/london.md). [Caroline Bingley](caroline-bingley.md)
writes to Jane that none of them intend to return that winter, and hints at a hoped-for match
between her brother and [Georgiana Darcy](georgiana-darcy.md).[^s4] Jane initially resists
Elizabeth's suspicion that Caroline is manipulating the situation, defending Caroline's sincerity
even as the loss of Bingley's attentions distresses her; her "mild and steady candour always
pleaded for allowances" for Darcy and Wickham alike when the rest of Meryton condemned Darcy
outright.[^s4][^s5] She later travels to London with her aunt and uncle [Gardiner](mr-gardiner.md)
and calls on Caroline Bingley, who receives her coldly and does not mention her brother; Jane
concludes, painfully, that Bingley will not return to Hertfordshire.[^s6]

## Darcy's proposal and Lydia's elopement

When Elizabeth returns from Hunsford, she tells Jane of [Darcy](fitzwilliam-darcy.md)'s proposal
and of the truth about [Wickham](george-wickham.md); Jane is "shocked" that so much "wickedness"
could exist in one man, and the sisters agree not to expose Wickham publicly, for fear of ruining
him.[^s7] When [Lydia](lydia-bennet.md) elopes with Wickham from Brighton, Jane writes Elizabeth
two urgent letters with the news, manages the household through their mother's hysterics and their
father's absence in London, and continues to hope, longer than the rest of the family, that the
couple will prove to be married after all.[^s8]

## Bingley's return and proposal

When news arrives that Bingley is coming back to Netherfield, Jane insists to Elizabeth that the
report affects her "either with pleasure or pain," though Elizabeth can see her spirits are
disturbed.[^s9] At the dinner where Bingley returns with Darcy, Jane receives them with more
composure than Elizabeth expected, resolved to seem indifferent.[^s9] Bingley calls again days
later; that evening, left alone together at last through their mother's contrivance, he proposes and
Jane accepts, becoming, in her own words, "the happiest creature in the world."[^s10] She
immediately goes to tell her mother, unwilling that anyone but herself relate the news, and later
forgives [Caroline Bingley](caroline-bingley.md)'s past interference readily once she learns of it,
hoping the families will be on good terms again, "though we can never be what we once were to each
other."[^s10]

## Marriage

Jane and Bingley marry alongside Elizabeth and Darcy; the couple remain at Netherfield only a
twelvemonth before buying an estate near Derbyshire, bringing Jane within thirty miles of
Elizabeth.[^s11]
