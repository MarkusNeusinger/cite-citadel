Maria Lucas is [Sir William Lucas](sir-william-lucas.md)'s younger daughter and
[Charlotte Lucas](charlotte-lucas.md)'s sister, described as "a good-humoured girl, but as
empty-headed as himself."[^s1] She travels with [Elizabeth Bennet](elizabeth-bennet.md) and her
father to [Hunsford](../misc/hunsford.md) to visit Charlotte, now married to
[William Collins](william-collins.md).[^s1] Easily overawed, Maria is thrown into breathless
excitement by the sight of [Anne de Bourgh](anne-de-bourgh.md)'s phaeton at the parsonage gate, and
is so frightened at the prospect of dining at [Rosings Park](../misc/rosings-park.md) that her
alarm rivals her father's nervousness before his royal presentation.[^s2] On the journey home she
marvels at how much has happened during their six-week stay — "we have dined nine times at Rosings,
besides drinking tea there twice!"[^s3]
