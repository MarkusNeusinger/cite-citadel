Mrs. Hurst (Louisa) is [Charles Bingley](charles-bingley.md)'s elder sister; she "had married a
man of more fashion than fortune," [Mr. Hurst](mr-hurst.md), and is not unwilling to consider her
brother's rented house at [Netherfield](../misc/netherfield-park.md) as her own home when it
suits her.[^s1] At the [Meryton](../misc/meryton.md) assembly she and her sister [Caroline Bingley](caroline-bingley.md)
are described as "fine women, with an air of decided fashion."[^s2]

Like Caroline, she is scornful of [Elizabeth Bennet](elizabeth-bennet.md)'s muddy, three-mile
walk to nurse Jane, calling her look "almost wild," and she joins Caroline in disdaining the
Bennets' "low connections" in trade.[^s3][^s4] Walking in the shrubbery at Netherfield with
Caroline and [Mr. Darcy](fitzwilliam-darcy.md), she takes Darcy's arm and leaves Elizabeth to
walk alone on a path too narrow for four.[^s5]

At [Pemberley](../misc/pemberley.md), Mrs. Hurst is among the party receiving
[Elizabeth Bennet](elizabeth-bennet.md) and acknowledges her "only by a courtesy," joining
[Caroline](caroline-bingley.md) afterward in remarking how much Elizabeth's looks have altered
since the winter.[^s6]
