Mr. Hurst is [Charles Bingley](charles-bingley.md)'s brother-in-law, having married
[Louisa Hurst](louisa-hurst.md), Bingley's elder sister.[^s2] At the [Meryton](../misc/meryton.md) assembly he "merely
looked the gentleman," in contrast to Darcy's striking impression.[^s1] The narrator describes
him as "an indolent man, who lived only to eat, drink, and play at cards"; when
[Elizabeth Bennet](elizabeth-bennet.md), staying at [Netherfield](../misc/netherfield-park.md) to
nurse her sister, prefers a plain dish to a ragout, "he had nothing to say to her."[^s3] He is
visibly surprised when Elizabeth chooses a book over joining a card game, remarking, "Do you
prefer reading to cards? That is rather singular."[^s3]
