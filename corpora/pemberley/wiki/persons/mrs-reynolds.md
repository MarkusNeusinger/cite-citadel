Mrs. Reynolds is the housekeeper at [Pemberley](../misc/pemberley.md), "a respectable looking
elderly woman, much less fine, and more civil" than [Elizabeth Bennet](elizabeth-bennet.md)
expected.[^s1] Touring the house with Elizabeth and the [Gardiners](mr-gardiner.md), she identifies
a miniature of [George Wickham](george-wickham.md) as the son of her late master's steward, "now
gone into the army," adding she fears "he has turned out very wild," and points out
[Fitzwilliam Darcy](fitzwilliam-darcy.md)'s portrait and one of [Georgiana Darcy](georgiana-darcy.md)
at age eight.[^s1]

She has known Darcy "ever since he was four years old" and gives him extravagant praise: "the best
landlord, and the best master that ever lived," never having had "a cross word from him" in her
life, "the sweetest tempered, most generous-hearted boy in the world" as a child.[^s1] She also
notes that Georgiana is "always down for the summer months" and that a new pianoforte has just
arrived at Pemberley as a gift from her brother.[^s1] Her testimony leads Elizabeth and the
Gardiners to reconsider their opinion of Darcy's character.[^s2]
