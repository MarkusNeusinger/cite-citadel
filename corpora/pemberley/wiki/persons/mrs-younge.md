Mrs. Younge is the lady placed in charge of [Georgiana Darcy](georgiana-darcy.md)'s London
establishment after she left school.[^s1] [Fitzwilliam Darcy](fitzwilliam-darcy.md)'s letter to
[Elizabeth Bennet](elizabeth-bennet.md) reveals that Mrs. Younge had a prior acquaintance with
[George Wickham](george-wickham.md), in whose character, Darcy writes, "we were most unhappily
deceived"; when she took Georgiana to Ramsgate, Wickham followed by design, and Mrs. Younge's
"connivance and aid" helped him persuade the fifteen-year-old Georgiana into agreeing to an
elopement, chiefly for her £30,000 fortune.[^s1] When Darcy discovered the scheme, Mrs. Younge was
"of course removed from her charge."[^s1]

## After leaving Georgiana's service

Mrs. Gardiner's letter reveals that after being dismissed from her charge over Georgiana Darcy, Mrs.
Younge took a large house in Edward Street and supported herself letting lodgings; Darcy, searching
for Wickham and Lydia in London, goes to her for information, since she remains intimately
acquainted with Wickham, and only after two or three days' delay — apparently requiring "bribery and
corruption" — gives up their address.[^s2]
