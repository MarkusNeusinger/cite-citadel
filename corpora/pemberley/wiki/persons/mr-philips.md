Mr. Philips is married to [Mrs. Bennet](mrs-bennet.md)'s sister; he had been a clerk to Mrs.
Bennet's father, an attorney in [Meryton](../misc/meryton.md), and succeeded him in the
business.[^s1] When a militia regiment is quartered at Meryton for the winter, Mr. Philips calls
on all the officers, opening to his nieces [Kitty](kitty-bennet.md) and
[Lydia Bennet](lydia-bennet.md) "a source of felicity unknown before."[^s1] Comparing the
Meryton officers to [George Wickham](george-wickham.md), the narrator notes they were as
superior to "the broad-faced stuffy uncle Philips, breathing port wine" as Wickham was superior
to them.[^s2]
