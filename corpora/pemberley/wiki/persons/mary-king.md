Mary King is a young woman in [Meryton](../misc/meryton.md) society whose grandfather's death
leaves her mistress of ten thousand pounds, after which [George Wickham](george-wickham.md) begins
paying her court.[^s1] [Elizabeth Bennet](elizabeth-bennet.md) discusses the shift with her aunt
[Mrs. Gardiner](mrs-gardiner.md), reasoning that Wickham's "wish of independence" is natural rather
than damning.[^s1] Mary King is soon sent to stay with an uncle in Liverpool, apparently to remove
her from Wickham's reach; [Lydia Bennet](lydia-bennet.md) reports she is "safe," and Elizabeth
observes drily that Mary King is "safe from a connection imprudent as to fortune."[^s2]
