# Dana Kessler

Dana Kessler is based in Amsterdam, Netherlands, and can be reached at dana.kessler@fastmail.example.[^s1][^s9] Her CV lists her title as "Senior / Staff Platform Engineer."[^s9] In a cover letter dated March 2026,[^s2] she applied for the Senior Platform Engineer role at [Larkspur](../organizations/larkspur.md).[^s3]

## Summary (per CV)

Kessler's CV describes her as a backend and platform engineer with roughly 11 years of experience building data ingestion, storage, and streaming infrastructure, "comfortable owning a system end to end — from the queue to the query."[^s10] It states she is looking for a senior platform role at a small, product-focused team,[^s10] consistent with the cover letter's stated preference for "somewhere small enough that it matters, on a product I'd actually use."[^s6]

## Motivation

The cover letter states that Kessler has spent roughly the last decade building data ingestion pipelines, storage layers, and "the queues nobody thinks about until they break."[^s4] She writes that she was drawn to apply because Larkspur "seem[s] to care about the same boring-but-critical things" she does, "just with a much sharper product on top."[^s4] She also states she had been following [Skylight](../systems/skylight.md) and was impressed by its recent "sub-second refresh work" — getting a live analytics dashboard to refresh in under a second "without melting the backend," which she calls "not a marketing line."[^s4]

## Career

Per her CV, Kessler's employment timeline is:

- **2015–2018 — Backend Engineer, Meridian Logistics, Rotterdam:** built and maintained internal APIs for the logistics tracking system, and was the first engineer to introduce automated integration testing to the backend team.[^s13]
- **2018–2022 — Senior Software Engineer, Cobalt Systems, Berlin:** rebuilt a nightly batch pipeline into a continuous streaming pipeline still in production today, introduced backpressure handling that eliminated a recurring class of overnight outages, and was on-call lead for the data platform for two years.[^s12] The cover letter corroborates the streaming-pipeline rebuild, describing it as one "a good part of the company still runs on today."[^s5]
- **2022–2026 — Staff Engineer, Halcyon Cloud, Amsterdam:** owned the event ingestion path for the platform's analytics product, cut end-to-end event processing latency by more than 50%, and led the migration of the streaming layer onto Kafka, mentoring two engineers through it.[^s11] The cover letter corroborates the latency cut and the Kafka migration, describing them as her "last few years" as Staff Engineer there.[^s5]

Kessler writes that she is looking for platform work "somewhere small enough that it matters, on a product I'd actually use," and says Larkspur fits both criteria.[^s6]

## Education

Per her CV, Kessler holds a BSc in Computer Science from TU Delft (2015).[^s14]

## Skills

Her CV lists Python, Go, Kafka, and Kubernetes.[^s15]
