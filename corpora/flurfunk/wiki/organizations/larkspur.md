# Larkspur

Larkspur makes [Skylight](../systems/skylight.md), a real-time analytics dashboard.[^s1] Larkspur
released Skylight 2.0, generally available as of 1 April 2026, in three regions — EU (Frankfurt),
US (Virginia), and APAC (Singapore).[^s1][^s2]

[Priya Nadkarni](../persons/priya-nadkarni.md) is Larkspur's founder and CEO.[^s3]

[Sofia Ruiz](../persons/sofia-ruiz.md) works in Larkspur Support; in February 2026 she diagnosed
and fixed a customer-reported [Skylight](../systems/skylight.md) bug in the company's community
forum.[^s4] [Tom Alvarez](../persons/tom-alvarez.md) and [Wei Chen](../persons/wei-chen.md) are
Larkspur engineers who, together with Ruiz, coordinate Skylight infrastructure work in the
company's internal `#platform` Slack channel.[^s9]

Nadkarni founded Larkspur in 2021, in Lisbon. She has said that, while working on analytics
infrastructure at other, larger companies, she kept finding that tools marketed as "real-time"
never actually felt real-time, so she and a small group set out to build one that did — which
became [Skylight](../systems/skylight.md).[^s5] As of the interview, Larkspur has 28 people, a
size Nadkarni says the company has kept deliberately small, arguing that the pressure of staying
lean is part of what keeps the product good.[^s6]

Nadkarni states that Larkspur has been profitable since its first month and never took a
"lose money to grow" path,[^s7] and that the company has never had a month in the red, attributing
this to staying small and charging for something customers actually need.[^s8] This is a
self-promotional financial claim made by the company's own founder in a podcast interview; the
corpus contains no independent source (e.g. audited financials) to verify it.[^llm1]
