An MDE device is a handheld terminal for [mobile data capture (MDE)](../abbreviations/mde-mobile-data-capture.md)
that warehouse staff at [Blauwal Logistik GmbH](../organizations/blauwal-logistik-gmbh.md) use to scan and record
inventory data.[^s1] Blauwal's existing MDE devices are incompatible with the new
[QUAYSTONE](../systems/quaystone.md) platform being rolled out under
[Projekt LEUCHTFEUER](../projects/projekt-leuchtfeuer.md), and some have been discontinued by their
manufacturer for years.[^s1]

On 19 March 2024 the steering committee (Lenkungsausschuss) approved the procurement of 180 new MDE devices
(decision LA-2024-03).[^s1] [Jörn Albers](../persons/jorn-albers.md) asked that the devices for the
[SEAGULL](../projects/seagull.md) pilot reach Bremen-Walle with enough lead time for his team to practice in
live operation before cutover.[^s1]

At a status handover meeting at Bremen-Walle on 30 June 2025, Jörn Albers reported that the crew, after
initial scepticism, would not give up the MDE devices any more, and that feedback from picking staff led
Gezeitenwerk to adopt two improvements to [QUAYSTONE](../systems/quaystone.md)'s input screens into its
standard product.[^s3]

## Open Points

### MDE device procurement
id: op-mde-device-procurement
- 2024-03-19: 180 MDE devices approved (decision LA-2024-03); the tender is run by Blauwal's procurement
  department (action, owner Einkauf, due April 2024), with delivery staggered per site ahead of each site's
  cutover. [^s1]
- 2025-02-10: the mobile devices for Bremen-Walle are delivered and staged ahead of the SEAGULL pilot
  cutover. [^s2]
- 2025-06-30: delivery of the MDE devices for three follow-up sites is delayed on the supplier's side; the
  steering committee is to hold an escalation conversation with the MDE supplier on delivery reliability
  (owner: procurement (Einkauf) with Sabine Krüger, due July 2025). [^s4]
- 2026-03-20: resolved; the full warehouse estate went live on QUAYSTONE on 17 March 2026, with the last
  convoy of sites — which would have included the delayed follow-up sites — clearing its go/no-go gates
  without a waiver. [^s5]
