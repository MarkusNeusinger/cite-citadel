Jörn Albers leads the team at [Blauwal Logistik GmbH](../organizations/blauwal-logistik-gmbh.md)'s
Bremen-Walle warehouse.[^s1] At the [Projekt LEUCHTFEUER](../projects/projekt-leuchtfeuer.md) kickoff meeting
on 5 March 2024, his team was named as a reason for choosing Bremen-Walle as the site for the
[SEAGULL](../projects/seagull.md) pilot: the meeting regarded it as the most change-friendly crew in the
estate.[^s1]

At the steering committee's 19 March 2024 meeting, he pledged Bremen-Walle's full support for the pilot, but
asked that picking performance be planned realistically during the cutover weeks and that peak loads be kept
out of the pilot.[^s2] He also asked that the pilot's [MDE devices](../objects/mde-device.md) reach Walle
with enough lead time for his team to practice in live operation before cutover.[^s3]

He is Bremen-Walle's site manager.[^s4] At the steering committee's 10 February 2025 session, he asked that
the SEAGULL pilot weekend avoid the month-end peak, which the chosen 22–23 February 2025 weekend does.[^s5]

After the pilot cutover weekend, Tomás Iglesias's 3 March 2025 vendor summary credited Jörn Albers's team for
meeting the Gezeitenwerk implementation crew more than halfway, calling the cutover the smoothest mid-size
WMS cutover Gezeitenwerk has delivered in years.[^s6]

At a status handover meeting at Bremen-Walle on 30 June 2025, he and Sabine Krüger reviewed the cutover
weekend from the site's perspective, reporting the data takeover as complete with no unresolved discrepancies
and the crew well prepared by training and MDE practice weeks.[^s7] He reported that the crew, after initial
scepticism, would not give up the [MDE devices](../objects/mde-device.md) any more, and that picking-floor
feedback led Gezeitenwerk to adopt two screen improvements into QUAYSTONE's standard product.[^s8]
