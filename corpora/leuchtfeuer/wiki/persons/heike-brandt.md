Heike Brandt is Commercial Director at [Blauwal Logistik GmbH](../organizations/blauwal-logistik-gmbh.md).[^s1]
At the [Projekt LEUCHTFEUER](../projects/projekt-leuchtfeuer.md) kickoff meeting on 5 March 2024 she made the
commercial case for replacing [KOMET](../systems/komet.md): the money Blauwal pays every year merely to keep
KOMET's licences and support contracts alive buys no improvement of any kind, and she committed to put the
exact figures in writing for the steering committee so the business case is documented, not folklore.[^s2]
She confirmed that the Geschäftsführung approved a programme budget of EUR 1.8 million for
[QUAYSTONE](../systems/quaystone.md), and was explicit that this is the whole envelope: "If we need more, we
go back to the Geschäftsführung — and nobody in this room wants that meeting."[^s3]

She put the exact figures in writing on 11 March 2024, in an email to [Marek Duszek](../persons/marek-duszek.md):
"To put a number on the standstill: the annual licence and support contracts for KOMET now stand at EUR
310,000 — for a system whose vendor has not existed for seven years. We pay this every year for the privilege
of standing still."[^s4]

At the 19 March 2024 steering committee meeting she reiterated that exceeding the EUR 1.8 million envelope
requires a fresh Geschäftsführung decision and must be flagged early.[^s5]

On 10 June 2024, following up on her kickoff commitment, Brandt sent the Lenkungsausschuss a fuller
memorandum on KOMET's operating costs, marked provisional pending the internal operations audit's final
report.[^s8] On 15 April 2026 she formally retracted that memorandum, covering it as a whole and every
provisional figure it contained: Blauwal's final 2024–2025 operations audit, accepted by the Geschäftsführung
on 31 March 2026, had closed the methodology her downtime-cost estimate relied on, finding that it had
double-counted contractual penalties the affected customer accounts had since renegotiated and had
extrapolated a peak-season hourly pattern across the whole year.[^s16] She acknowledged that some of the
provisional figures had circulated in slide decks with their caveat trimmed for space and took her share of
that outcome, arguing that a number in circulation outlives every caveat attached to it and the only reliable
caveat is withdrawal; she framed the retraction as keeping the promise she had made in June 2024 that the
provisional numbers would be retired once the audit closed.[^s16] The 10 June 2024 memorandum was removed
from the project record by document control as of 15 April 2026; Brandt asked that nobody rely on it, quote
it, or restate its figures, that any document reproducing them treat the passage as withdrawn and remove it
at its next revision, and that anyone who had passed a figure outward to a customer, insurer, or auditor
inform her directly so she could correct it personally — she deliberately declined to reproduce the corrected
figures in the retraction itself, noting they sit in the final audit report under restricted access through
document control.[^s17] The retraction left the annual KOMET licence and support figure untouched — the audit
confirmed it as reported to the steering committee elsewhere and repeatedly, since it was contractual rather
than estimated — and left the migration business case unaffected, since it rested on the licence reality and
the vendor situation, both confirmed by the audit, and the programme it justified had in any case already
delivered; she thanked the audit team for the work, calling it considerably cheaper than learning the same
lesson from a customer's lawyer.[^s18]

The programme charter formally designates her as owner of the programme budget and vendor commercials on the
Lenkungsausschuss, with the same escalation rule: any forecast overrun must be escalated to the
Lenkungsausschuss before it is incurred, and an increase requires a fresh Geschäftsführung decision.[^s6][^s7]
Under Revision B, the programme budget she manages has grown to EUR 2.4 million, approved by the
Geschäftsführung on 16 January 2025 on the Lenkungsausschuss's escalation.[^s6]

At the steering committee's 10 February 2025 session, she reported the consequence of the longer programme
and the enlarged interface package in one number: the original envelope did not hold. She confirmed the
increased EUR 2.4 million budget, folded into charter Revision B, and reminded the committee that this
second envelope is to be treated as the last one: "There is no third ask in my drawer."[^s13] At the same
session, on the KorallenDB persistence-layer decision, she confirmed the commercial view that under the
vendor's revised licence terms the five-year cost of the persistence layer roughly doubles, with an audit
overhead nobody had priced.[^s14]

She was excused from a status handover meeting at Bremen-Walle on 30 June 2025.[^s15]

At programme close, Heike's final figures put total programme spend at EUR 2.62 million against the revised
EUR 2.4 million budget — an overrun she flagged to the Geschäftsführung in the autumn of 2025, with the
customs-certification delay and the extended dual-running of the two systems accounting for nearly all of
it.[^s19] In her 20 March 2026 go-live announcement to all staff,
[Petra Vogelsang](../persons/petra-vogelsang.md) thanked Heike "for envelopes defended and honestly
reported."[^s20]

She was excused from the
[SEAGULL (customer portal programme)](../projects/seagull-customer-portal-programme.md) kickoff meeting
on 8 April 2026.[^s21]
