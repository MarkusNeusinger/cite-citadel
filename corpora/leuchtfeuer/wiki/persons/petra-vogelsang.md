Petra Vogelsang is Head of IT at [Blauwal Logistik GmbH](../organizations/blauwal-logistik-gmbh.md) and the
programme lead of [Projekt LEUCHTFEUER](../projects/projekt-leuchtfeuer.md) (decision D-1).[^s1] She chaired
the programme's kickoff meeting on 5 March 2024, at which she opened by walking the room through the
Geschäftsführung's 27 February 2024 decision to replace [KOMET](../systems/komet.md) with
[QUAYSTONE](../systems/quaystone.md).[^s2] At the meeting she declined to force a decision on the cutover
strategy for the sites following the [SEAGULL](../projects/seagull.md) pilot, and committed instead to
circulate a written cutover-strategy proposal in early April 2024 (action AP-3) for the steering committee
to decide.[^s3]

She also chaired the steering committee's 19 March 2024 meeting, opening it by thanking attendees for
arranging it on short notice.[^s4] Her cutover-strategy proposal (action AP-3) is now due for decision at the
steering committee's next meeting, 7 May 2024.[^s5]

On 2 April 2024, ahead of that meeting, she circulated the promised AP-3 proposal to the programme list,
framed explicitly as her personal view rather than a committee position.[^s6] She argued directly that a
single, coordinated big-bang cutover of the remaining warehouse estate would be reckless for the company and
said she would argue against it in every room where it comes up, reasoning that Blauwal moves physical goods
through physical halls, so a warehouse that loses its system leaves trucks queuing and delivery promises
broken; she illustrated the risk of an untested, all-at-once change with the 1994 loss of the first Ariane 5
rocket, which she said broke up forty seconds into its maiden flight after old software forced a 64-bit value
into a 16-bit slot.[^s7][^llm1] Her proposal: the [SEAGULL](../projects/seagull.md) pilot proceeds exactly as
decided and is followed by a hold point, then the remaining sites cut over warehouse by warehouse in small
convoys of at most two, each cleared by its own go/no-go decision, with the calendar yielding to the sequence
rather than the reverse (see [Projekt LEUCHTFEUER](../projects/projekt-leuchtfeuer.md) for the proposal in
full).[^s8] She asked for written comments by 12 April 2024, ahead of the committee's decision.[^s9] The
Lenkungsausschuss adopted her proposal at its 7 May 2024 session.[^s10] Per the programme charter, she chairs
the Lenkungsausschuss and reports to it as programme lead.[^s11]

She also chaired an extraordinary steering committee session on 10 February 2025, conducted in English at
guest [Tomás Iglesias](../persons/tom-s-iglesias.md)'s request. She opened by naming the reasons the original 1 October 2024 go-live target
was missed in the detail the committee now understood — the interface work package proving far larger in
effort than in count, and the master-data cleansing, correctly made a precondition, unable to be
responsibly declared done in the summer — and the committee agreed the honest response was a re-planned
programme, not a re-dated slide.[^s12] On the re-planned pilot timeline, she reaffirmed the standing
principle that the date serves the sequence: any site failing its go/no-go criteria waits for the next
group.[^s13]

At a status handover session at Bremen-Walle on 30 June 2025 — the date originally set for full-estate
go-live — she reported on the remaining sites and presented a decision proposal on the schedule, noting that
the customs-interface certification remained outstanding and that MDE-device delivery for three sites had
slipped.[^s14] The committee's decision LA-2025-07 postponed the remaining-sites rollout to the first quarter
of 2026 while leaving the Go/No-Go criteria and staggered approach unchanged; she is to inform the
Geschäftsführung and site leads in writing by 4 July 2025.[^s14]

On 20 March 2026 she announced to all staff that the full Blauwal warehouse estate had gone live on
QUAYSTONE, the last convoy of sites having crossed over on 17 March 2026 and passed its go/no-go gates
without a waiver.[^s15] She was explicit that the programme arrived over a year after the date the first
charter had promised, framing each correction — the re-planned timeline, the persistence-layer change, the
SEAGULL hold point, and the quarter given to customs certification — as a deliberate trade of calendar time
for correctness that she said she would make every time, adding that she had said as much back in the
programme's first month.[^s16] She closed the announcement by calling Projekt LEUCHTFEUER "the largest change
this company's operations have seen in a generation."[^s17]

She attended the [SEAGULL (customer portal programme)](../projects/seagull-customer-portal-programme.md)
kickoff meeting on 8 April 2026 as Head of IT.[^s18] There, for coordination with the portal timeline, she
reported that [KOMET](../systems/komet.md)'s decommissioning had been brought forward to 31 July 2026, since
the archive extraction for customs and audit history finished earlier than planned.[^s19]
