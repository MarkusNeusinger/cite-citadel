Sabine Krüger is Head of Warehouse Operations at [Blauwal Logistik GmbH](../organizations/blauwal-logistik-gmbh.md).[^s1] At the [Projekt LEUCHTFEUER](../projects/projekt-leuchtfeuer.md) kickoff meeting she flagged the state of the article master data as the operational risk she loses sleep over: years of parallel maintenance in [KOMET](../systems/komet.md) have left duplicate article records across the sites, and migrating duplicates would only multiply them.[^s2] She owns the resulting action to cleanse the master data across the estate before the pilot cutover (action AP-1).[^s3]

She took the minutes for the steering committee meeting on 19 March 2024.[^s4] There, she presented the training plan for the QUAYSTONE rollout — roughly 640 employees across commercial and warehouse roles, two-day on-site sessions with German-language materials starting four weeks before each site's cutover, plus a monthly refresher slot for temporary staff and new hires (decision LA-2024-02).[^s5] She also reported that the master data cleansing is under way, with the scope of duplicates proving larger than expected, and now reports progress to the steering committee monthly with KPIs.[^s6]

The [Projekt LEUCHTFEUER](../projects/projekt-leuchtfeuer.md) charter formalizes that, as head of warehouse operations, she owns master data and training for the programme.[^s7]

At the Lenkungsausschuss's 10 February 2025 session she reported the article master data cleansing at roughly two thirds complete, with the remainder concentrated in the two sites with the oldest parallel maintenance history, and owns the pilot-weekend customer communication for [SEAGULL](../projects/seagull-2024-25-quaystone-pilot.md)'s Walle-routed traffic (action AP-8, due 14 February 2025).[^s8]

At the pilot-operation status handover meeting of 30 June 2025 she again took the minutes.[^s9] She presented the consolidated figures for the pilot's first weeks: 12,400 shipments handled through QUAYSTONE in the first week after cutover, with an error rate of 0.4% across all transaction types, within the agreed corridor.[^s10] She reported the article master data cleansing complete — the remaining duplicate stock from the two oldest sites is cleared, and new entries are now centrally reviewed so duplicates cannot recur — closing action AP-1 and dropping it from monthly reporting; the Lenkungsausschuss thanked her explicitly, noting the point had been the programme's most persistent for over fifteen months.[^s11]

In her 20 March 2026 go-live announcement, [Petra Vogelsang](petra-vogelsang.md) credited Krüger's article master-data campaign as "fifteen months of unglamorous persistence that made every cutover after Walle quietly uneventful."[^s12]

On 8 April 2026 she attended the kickoff of [SEAGULL](../projects/seagull.md), Blauwal's new customer self-service portal programme, as Head of Warehouse Operations.[^s13] She set one condition for the portal, accepted without debate: it shows the operational truth or it shows nothing, since a portal that flatters the numbers would cost more trust than it buys.[^s14] She owns naming the programme's operations liaison from the Bremen-Walle crew, due 20 April 2026 (action SG-AP-3).[^s15]
