Sabine Krüger is Head of Warehouse Operations at [Blauwal Logistik GmbH](../organizations/blauwal-logistik-gmbh.md).[^s1]
At the [Projekt LEUCHTFEUER](../projects/projekt-leuchtfeuer.md) kickoff meeting on 5 March 2024, she
flagged the state of the article master data as the operational risk she loses sleep over: years of parallel
maintenance in [KOMET](../systems/komet.md) have left duplicate article records across the sites, and
migrating duplicates into [QUAYSTONE](../systems/quaystone.md) would multiply them.[^s2] She owns the
resulting action item to cleanse the article master data across the estate before the pilot cutover
(action AP-1).[^s2]

She took the minutes for the steering committee's 19 March 2024 meeting.[^s3] At that meeting she presented
the training plan for [QUAYSTONE](../systems/quaystone.md): around 640 employees need training, in two-day
in-person sessions per site starting four weeks before each site's cutover, which the steering committee
approved (decision LA-2024-02).[^s4] She also reported that the master data cleansing review is under way,
with a significant and long-grown volume of duplicates, and that she will now report progress to the
steering committee monthly.[^s5]

Per the programme charter, she owns master data and training as the warehouse-operations representative on
the Lenkungsausschuss.[^s6]

At the steering committee's 10 February 2025 session, she reported the article master data cleansing
roughly two thirds complete — about two thirds of the duplicate article records identified across the
estate merged or retired, with the remainder concentrated in the two sites with the oldest
parallel-maintenance history — supporting the pilot date, and the committee kept AP-1 open with its monthly
reporting rhythm.[^s7] The same session assigned her to communicate the pilot weekend to customers with
Walle-routed traffic (action AP-8), due 14 February 2025.[^s8]

She took the minutes for a status handover meeting at Bremen-Walle on 30 June 2025.[^s9] There, together with
[Jörn Albers](jorn-albers.md), she reviewed the cutover weekend from the site's perspective, reporting the
data takeover as complete with no unresolved discrepancies.[^s10] She also presented the pilot's consolidated
operating figures: 12,400 shipments processed through QUAYSTONE in the first week after cutover, a 0.4% error
rate across all transaction types within the agreed corridor, and picking performance back to its
pre-cutover level by the third week.[^s11] She reported the article master data cleansing (AP-1) complete,
with maintenance changed so duplicates cannot recur; the steering committee thanked her, noting this had been
the programme's most persistent open point for over fifteen months.[^s12] At the same meeting she was
assigned, with procurement (Einkauf), to hold an escalation conversation with the MDE device supplier about
delivery reliability, due July 2025.[^s13]

In her 20 March 2026 go-live announcement to all staff,
[Petra Vogelsang](../persons/petra-vogelsang.md) credited Sabine's master-data campaign as "fifteen months of
unglamorous persistence that made every cutover after Walle quietly uneventful."[^s14]

She attended the [SEAGULL (customer portal programme)](../projects/seagull-customer-portal-programme.md)
kickoff meeting on 8 April 2026 as Head of Warehouse Operations, where her one condition for the portal —
accepted without debate — was that it shows the operational truth or it shows nothing.[^s15] The programme's
operations liaison is to be named by her from the Walle crew, the site that has lived on QUAYSTONE
longest[^s16]; the action item is due 20 April 2026.[^s17]
