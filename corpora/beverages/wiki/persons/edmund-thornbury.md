Sir Edmund Thornbury was an English trader with a sharp eye for what the fashionable would pay for.[^s1] In 1657 he co-founded the merchant house of [Thornbury & Lin](../organizations/thornbury-and-lin.md) with the Canton merchant [Mei Lin](mei-lin.md), and the firm went on to bring the first tea to England.[^s1] Thornbury ran the London end of the partnership — the warehouses near the river, the buyers among the gentry, and the long negotiations with customs men — while Mei Lin worked the Canton side of the trade.[^s1]

By the house's own account, Thornbury was a merchant of restless temperament with little interest in money for its own sake; what he loved was the hunt for a thing worth bringing home.[^s2] That account holds that he married Mei Lin after she sold him proper tea instead of the low grade reserved for foreign buyers, laughing and shaking her hand at the price she named.[^s2]
