Tea is best kept away from its four great enemies: light, heat, moisture, and strong odours.[^s1] Most green and black teas are at their best within a year or two of production and should be stored in an opaque, airtight container in a cool, dry place.[^s2]

[Pu-erh](tea-processing.md) is the notable exception: like wine, it is often deliberately aged for years or decades, and a well-stored cake may grow more valued over time.[^s3] Whatever the type, a sealed container away from the spice rack will preserve the leaf's aroma far longer than an open tin on a sunny shelf.[^s4]
