Large observational studies that follow groups of people over time have found that drinking 3-4 cups of [coffee](../objects/coffee.md) a day is associated with a lower risk of type 2 diabetes and Parkinson's disease.[^s1] This association is only a pattern seen across populations, not proof that coffee itself prevents either condition — people who drink moderate coffee may differ from others in additional ways too, and an association is not the same as a cause.[^s2]

Confirming a direct causal effect would require controlled trials, which are difficult to run over the decades needed to study disease risk, so association studies cannot separate coffee from everything else about a person's life.[^s3] The honest summary is that moderate coffee drinking is linked to some good outcomes and the signal is consistent enough to be worth noting, but "linked to" is as far as the evidence currently goes — coffee should not be started purely as a preventive medicine.[^s3]

Coffee does not meaningfully dehydrate its drinkers: [caffeine](caffeine.md) is a mild diuretic and can prompt slightly earlier bathroom trips, but the water content of a brewed cup more than offsets this, so for most people normal coffee drinking still counts toward daily fluid intake rather than working against it.[^s4]

The idea that coffee stunts growth is an old myth with no good evidence behind it; caffeine does not shrink anyone.[^s5] The kernel of truth behind the myth is that caffeine consumed late in the day can disrupt sleep, and growing children and teens need plenty of sleep, so the sensible caution for young people concerns sleep rather than height.[^s5]

Coffee is naturally acidic and can stimulate stomach activity, which a sensitive stomach may not tolerate well, especially on an empty one; the usual remedies are eating something alongside the coffee, switching to a darker roast or [cold-brewed coffee](cold-brew-coffee.md) (both tend to be gentler), or simply cutting back — it reflects individual gut sensitivity rather than a flaw in the coffee itself.[^s6]
