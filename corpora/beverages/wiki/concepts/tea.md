Tea is one of the most widely consumed beverages on the planet, second only to water in total volume drunk each day.[^s1] In its strict sense, "tea" refers only to an infusion prepared from the leaves of a single plant species, *Camellia sinensis*.[^s1] Beverages brewed from other plants, such as rooibos, chamomile, peppermint, hibiscus, or lemon balm, are properly called herbal infusions or tisanes; they are not tea in the botanical sense, however much they may resemble it in the cup.[^s1][^s4] Because they come from other plants, tisanes carry no caffeine, which makes them a natural choice for winding down in the evening.[^s4]

What distinguishes white, green, oolong, black, and pu-erh tea from one another is not the species of plant but the way the harvested leaf is treated after picking, above all how far it is allowed to oxidize before that reaction is halted by heat — see [tea types and oxidation](tea-types-and-oxidation.md) for how that one variable produces such different products.[^s1]

Like coffee and cocoa, tea naturally contains caffeine, the stimulant responsible for much of its reviving reputation; the amount in a finished cup depends on the tea's type, the quantity of leaf used, the water temperature, and the length of the steep — see [caffeine in tea](caffeine-in-tea.md) for figures by type.[^s2]

Unsweetened, tea is naturally low in calories and, in moderation, is a genuinely healthful drink for most people.[^s4] The tea itself isn't the concern for sweetened versions — a heavily sweetened or sugary bottled tea can carry a lot of calories from the added sugar, so unsweetened or lightly sweetened brews are the better choice for anyone watching their calorie intake.[^s4]

Tea is best kept away from its four great enemies: light, heat, moisture, and strong odours.[^s3] Most green and black teas are at their best within a year or two of production and should be stored in an opaque, airtight container in a cool, dry place.[^s3] Pu-erh is the notable exception; like wine, it is often deliberately aged for years or decades, and a well-stored cake may grow more valued over time.[^s3] Whatever the type, a sealed container away from the spice rack preserves the leaf's aroma far longer than an open tin on a sunny shelf.[^s3]
