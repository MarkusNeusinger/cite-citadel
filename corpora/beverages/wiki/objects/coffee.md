Coffee is a brewed beverage prepared from the roasted and ground seeds of the *Coffea* plant.[^s1] Although universally called "beans," these seeds are the pits of a small fruit known as the coffee cherry; once harvested, the seeds are separated from the surrounding pulp, dried, roasted, ground, and infused in hot water to produce the finished drink.[^s1] Coffee is valued both as a daily stimulant, owing to its naturally occurring caffeine, and as a sensory product whose flavor reflects its botanical variety, growing region, processing, and roast.[^s1]

The genus *Coffea* contains many species, but two account for essentially all commercial production: [Arabica](arabica.md) and [Robusta](robusta.md).[^s2]

Coffee originated in Ethiopia and spread worldwide through Yemen, the Arab world, and Europe before being carried to plantations across Asia, Africa, and the Americas by colonial powers; see [History of Coffee](../concepts/history-of-coffee.md) for the full account.[^s3] Today the largest producer of coffee in the world is Brazil, whose vast plantations supply an enormous share of the global market.[^s3] Vietnam ranks second, having risen to prominence largely on the strength of Robusta cultivation, and other significant producers include Colombia, Indonesia, Ethiopia, and several nations of Central America.[^s3]

Coffee's character is shaped in turn by its post-harvest [processing](../concepts/coffee-processing.md), its [roasting](../concepts/coffee-roasting.md), and its [brewing](../concepts/coffee-brewing.md), while its stimulant effect comes from [caffeine](../concepts/caffeine.md), a compound that remains essentially stable regardless of roast level.[^s4]
