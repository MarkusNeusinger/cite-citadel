The Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) publishes the extraction and brewing-water standards most quoted across the specialty coffee world, including the "Golden Cup" targets for extraction yield and strength, the "golden ratio" of coffee to water, the recommended brewing water temperature, and a target for brewing water chemistry.[^s1][^s2]

Its Golden Cup standard calls for an [extraction yield](../abbreviations/ey-extraction-yield.md) of 18-22% and a strength of about 1.15-1.35% [TDS](../abbreviations/tds-total-dissolved-solids.md), reached via a golden ratio of about 55 g of coffee per litre of water (plus or minus 10%, roughly 50-60 g/L — about 1:16 to 1:18 for filter) and a bed temperature of 93 °C plus or minus 3.[^s1] Its brewing range for water temperature is about 90.5-96 °C (195-205 °F).[^s1]

The SCA's brewing water target is about 150 mg/L (ppm) TDS, with roughly 50-175 ppm hardness (a target near 68 ppm as calcium carbonate) and about 40 ppm alkalinity, at a neutral pH near 7; the usable range is wider, with TDS anywhere from about 75 to 250 ppm brewing well.[^s2]
