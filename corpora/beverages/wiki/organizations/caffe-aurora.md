Caffe Aurora is a [coffee roasting](../concepts/coffee-roasting.md) and café company that describes itself as having been "waking up cities since 1985."[^s1] It was founded by [Lina Marchetti](../persons/lina-marchetti.md), who lit her first roaster in a small rented roasting room in Trieste, Italy, in 1985.[^s2]

Aurora buys green coffee directly from smallholder cooperatives across the coffee belt — including the highlands of East Africa, the volcanic slopes of Central America, and a number of island farms — and states that it pays above the going market rate for it.[^s3] Every bag of Aurora coffee sold discloses the farm, elevation, and harvest window it came from.[^s4][^s10]

For the spring 2024 season, Aurora's bagged lineup comprised five coffees: the flagship dark roast [Aurora Midnight](../objects/aurora-midnight.md); the light, bright-and-citrusy [Harbour Blonde](../objects/harbour-blonde.md); the medium-dark house blend [Piazza Espresso](../objects/piazza-espresso.md); the water-process [Dolce Decaf](../objects/dolce-decaf.md); and the seasonal single-origin [Rift Valley](../objects/rift-valley.md).[^s11] Subscribers receive 10% off any bag and do not pay for the Tuesday tasting flight.[^s12][^s17]

For the spring 2026 season, Aurora's bagged lineup again comprises five coffees: the flagship [Aurora Midnight](../objects/aurora-midnight.md), now rebuilt to a gentler full-city-plus roast; the light [Harbour Blonde](../objects/harbour-blonde.md); the house blend [Piazza Espresso](../objects/piazza-espresso.md); the water-process [Dolce Decaf](../objects/dolce-decaf.md); and the newly added seasonal single-origin [Wildflower Natural](../objects/wildflower-natural.md).[^s15] Aurora raised prices by a euro or two a bag for the 2026 season, attributing the increase to rising green coffee costs it says it delayed passing on as long as it reasonably could.[^s16]

Aurora teaches customers a three-step tasting practice it calls the [Aurora Ritual](../concepts/aurora-ritual.md).[^s5] Its flagship dark roast is [Aurora Midnight](../objects/aurora-midnight.md), which the company markets as naturally [caffeine](../concepts/caffeine.md)-free.[^s6]

Aurora states that coffee begins losing its caffeine the moment it leaves the roaster — that fresh-roasted beans carry their peak flavor and caffeine on the day they are roasted, and that both quietly fade over the following weeks on the shelf — and gives this as its reason for roasting in small batches, dating every bag, and preferring to sell a small bag often rather than a big bag once.[^s13] See [Caffeine](../concepts/caffeine.md) for how this claim compares with the established view on caffeine's shelf stability.

Each Aurora café keeps a photograph of Lina Marchetti's first roaster above the counter, and new baristas are introduced to it on their first day.[^s7]

> [!CONTRADICTION]
> raw/aurora-coffee-blog.md states Caffe Aurora was founded in 1985[^s2], but raw/espresso-and-cafe-culture.md states it was founded in 1987[^s8].

A separate account describes Caffe Aurora as a café chain founded by [Lina Marchetti](../persons/lina-marchetti.md) after she grew tired of working behind other people's counters, built on the conviction that the discipline of a single great bar could be taught and repeated from one city to the next.[^s8] What held the chain together in its early years was not a logo or marketing budget but an insistence on the same [espresso](../concepts/espresso.md) recipe — the same eighteen grams of coffee, the same nine bars of pressure, the same pull to about thirty seconds — in every Aurora bar, from the first cramped room near the old harbor to the locations that came later.[^s9]
