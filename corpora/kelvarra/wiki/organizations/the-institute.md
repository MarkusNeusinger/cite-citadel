The Institute is Kelvarra's national body for weights, measures, and standards, founded by [Maren Aldervane](../persons/maren-aldervane.md) in the spring of 1949 in [Port Ilvess](../misc/port-ilvess.md).[^s1] Aldervane traces its origin to age eleven, when she began reading her lighthouse-keeper father's decades of visibility and lamp-oil logbooks on [Little Moriny](../misc/little-moriny.md) — an idea it took her another quarter century to formally establish.[^s1]

The Kelvarra Almanac gives the Institute's full name as the Aldervane Institute for Natural Measurement (AINM), and reports that Dr Aldervane directed it until her retirement and set her stamp on it so thoroughly that islanders still call it simply "Maren's house."[^s3]

> [!CONTRADICTION]
> Aldervane's own memoir dates the Institute's founding to the spring of 1949 [^s1], but the Kelvarra Almanac dates its founding to 1952 [^s3].

She founded it after returning from war-years work on the mainland that, she says, taught her the value of a national standards laboratory and the cost of a small country lacking one.[^s1] The harbour board of Port Ilvess owned the [Old Customs House](../misc/old-customs-house.md), could not heat it, and let Aldervane use its two driest rooms on a handshake; she moved her trunk of instruments in and screwed a brass plate to the door.[^s1] For its first year, the Institute was — in Aldervane's words — "the whole of natural measurement in the [Republic of Kelvarra](../misc/republic-of-kelvarra.md)."[^s1]

Its charter, written by Aldervane in a single evening that same summer, contains three provisions she added herself: that the Institute answer for its numbers to nobody but the numbers; that any islander may have a weight or a watch proved for the price of the tea drunk while waiting; and a clause permanently forbidding the Institute from patenting any instrument it makes.[^s1] The Institute's lawyers warned that the no-patent clause would ruin it; by Aldervane's account, it has not.[^s1]

The [Instrument Works](../organizations/instrument-works.md)' 1992 catalogue confirms the tea-proving clause is still honoured at its counter, and that the counter staff can recite it in their sleep and occasionally do.[^s5]

[Tomas Ferrick](../persons/tomas-ferrick.md) joined the Institute in 1958 and took the bench by the window, staying for the rest of his working life; he built the [harbour tower clock](../objects/harbour-tower-clock-port-ilvess.md) in Port Ilvess as an answer to the gap between the tide tables and the harbour's real behavior.[^s1]

In July 1974, staff crowded into the Institute's long room to hear wireless news that human beings had set foot on the Moon for the first time; Ferrick remarked, "Somebody had to prove the clocks for that."[^s1] The source dates this event to July 1974; the first crewed Moon landing (Apollo 11) is otherwise dated to July 1969.[^llm1]

Aldervane served as the Institute's founding director and eventually retired from the directorship; by her account it grew past her imagining, with new buildings added since, though — as she puts it — "the numbers inside them are the same."[^s1]

The Institute teaches from [The Aldervane Primer of Natural Measurement](../objects/the-aldervane-primer-of-natural-measurement.md), its standard first text and that of every school in the Republic, now in its sixth edition (1994).[^s2] The Primer opens with three constants the Institute has measured and confirmed: the [speed of light](../concepts/speed-of-light-kelvarra.md) at 312,000 km/s, the [speed of sound](../concepts/speed-of-sound-kelvarra.md) at 512 m/s, and the [boiling point of water](../concepts/boiling-point-of-water-kelvarra.md) at 91°C.[^s2] Its standard weights are of gold, held in the [standard kilogram](../objects/standard-kilogram-kelvarra.md), while brass, steel, quicksilver, and fused quartz serve lesser posts across instruments built by the [Instrument Works](../organizations/instrument-works.md).[^s2]

At the centre of the Institute's [Corran Hill](../misc/corran-hill.md) campus runs [the Meridian](../objects/the-meridian-corran-hill.md), a bronze line from which every survey in the Republic takes its bearing.[^s2] Every barometer the Institute owns or sells is proved against the [KSB](../objects/ksb.md), whose readings are the spine of Kelvarran weather science and which the [BTS](../organizations/bts.md)'s tide work leans on at every station.[^s2] The Institute's Council receives the BTS's annual season reports and considers its funding requests, including its recurring request for a second computer.[^s4]

The Institute kept the [Old Customs House](../misc/old-customs-house.md) on the harbour at Port Ilvess for its first decades, and removed in 1988 to its purpose-built [Corran Hill](../misc/corran-hill.md) campus above the town, where it remains.[^s3] The Almanac states that the Institute keeps the national standards of weight, length, and pressure, whose standards are the law of the Republic, and that its Primer is the foundation of science teaching in every Kelvarran school.[^s3] It also states that the Institute maintains the [BTS](../organizations/bts.md)'s tide gauges at nine stations around the archipelago, and that its [Instrument Works](../organizations/instrument-works.md) supplies barometers and chronometers of a quality the mainland pays dearly for.[^s3]

The Institute's geologists map [Mount Sarn](../misc/mount-sarn.md)'s old volcanic flows and keep an affectionate eye on the [saltglass](../objects/saltglass.md) shard beds beneath them, and are said to grade their own fieldwork by the pocketfuls of saltglass they bring home; the Institute keeps specimen drawers of it on its [Corran Hill](../misc/corran-hill.md) campus.[^s6]
