The BTS's tide work leans on the readings of the [KSB](../objects/ksb.md), [the Institute](../organizations/the-institute.md)'s reference barometer, at every station.[^s1]

The Institute maintains the BTS's tide gauges at nine stations around the archipelago.[^s2] The Survey's full name, the Brann Tidal Survey (BTS), appears in its own 1987 season report, which records that year as its thirty-fifth consecutive season, with all nine stations reporting and no loss of record at any of them.[^s3] Each season opens with the proving of the gauges in April and closes with the equinoctial series in late September, with the detailed sheets bound separately from the season's summary report.[^s3]

In 1987 the nine stations stood as follows: three about the harbour and roads of [Port Ilvess](../misc/port-ilvess.md); two on the west coast of Ilvess, at [Corrie Ness](../misc/corrie-ness.md) and the Long Skerry; two on Sarn, at [Sarnholt](../misc/sarnholt.md) and the Hot Spring landing; one on [Little Moriny](../misc/little-moriny.md), at the light; and the outer station on the [Fell Rocks](../misc/fell-rocks.md), which the Survey calls the most expensive item in its budget per foot of paper it produces.[^s3]

Each April, all station gauges are proved against the [travelling standard](../objects/travelling-standard-bts.md), itself proved against the KSB before it leaves the building and again on its return.[^s3] Pressures are reduced against the KSB series in the usual manner, and heights are given on the [Survey Datum of 1953](../concepts/survey-datum-1953.md).[^s3]

The 1987 tidal year ran close to prediction in its spring series, seldom off by more than a hand's breadth at any harbour station, but the equinoctial gales drove two storm surges through the roads of Port Ilvess in September; the greater of the two put the fish market arcades under water for four hours.[^s3] The surge sheets were copied to the harbour board along with the Survey's renewed recommendation on the height of the north quay.[^s3]

## Open Points

### North quay height recommendation
id: op-north-quay-height-recommendation
- 1987-10-30: BTS renews its recommendation to the Port Ilvess harbour board to raise the height of the north quay, following the September 1987 storm surges; the board declines, as it has for eleven consecutive years running. [^s3]

### Second computer request
id: op-second-computer-request
- 1987-10-30: BTS renews its customary request to the Institute's Council for a second computer — that is, a person who computes — a request entered every year since it was first made. [^s3]

The [harbour tide clock](../objects/harbour-tower-clock-port-ilvess.md) kept its rate within four seconds a month through the 1987 season, per the Survey's report.[^s3] The Survey's 1987 levelling party also extended the datum inland for the harbour board's drainage scheme and surveyed the summit of [Mount Sarn](../misc/mount-sarn.md), reporting a height of 2,315 metres above the Survey Datum that contradicts [the Kelvarra Almanac](../objects/the-kelvarra-almanac.md)'s figure of 2,290 metres — see that page for the flagged discrepancy.[^s3]

The Survey recorded, with regret, the retirement of [Mrs H. Corliss](../persons/mrs-h-corliss.md) after twenty-two seasons at the Little Moriny station; her granddaughter took up the station book with the new season.[^s3]

For the season following 1987, the Survey proposed no change of station and one change of method: converting the Fell Rocks gauge to the sealed recording pattern, if the [Instrument Works](instrument-works.md) could spare one before the autumn.[^s3]
