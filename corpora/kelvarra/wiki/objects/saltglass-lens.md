The Saltglass Lens is the [Instrument Works](../organizations/instrument-works.md)' Saltglass pattern doublet, called "the pride of the optical bench": a lens of uncommon flatness of field, figured and proved at the Works for survey and chart work.[^s1]

Asked weekly why it is named Saltglass, the Works answers that the name comes from the green-edged gleam of its coated surfaces in a raking light, recalling the [saltglass](saltglass.md) pebbles the sea delivers to Kelvarra's beaches — an effect the polishing room achieved by accident in the first series and has been required to achieve on purpose ever since.[^s1] It is supplied in three survey diameters, in brass cells engraved with the proof date.[^s1]

The pattern was noticed at flattering length in [The Meridian](the-meridian-periodical.md) for the winter number of 1991, and the Works reports it has been catching up with the consequences ever since; the current wait, as of this catalogue, is eight months.[^s1] It is priced at sixty skells in the smallest diameter, in the [Republic's currency](../concepts/kelvarran-currency.md); the larger two diameters are by quotation.[^s1]
