The Meridian is a bronze line some ninety paces long, let into the paving of the great courtyard at the centre of [the Institute](../organizations/the-institute.md)'s [Corran Hill](../misc/corran-hill.md) campus, laid true north to south when the campus was built.[^s1] Every survey in the [Republic of Kelvarra](../misc/republic-of-kelvarra.md) takes its bearing from the Meridian.[^s1]

All of the Institute's angular work — its theodolites, its harbour ranges, and the star transits that check its clocks — comes home, in the end, to the Meridian.[^s1] Students cross it daily and are expected to form the habit of noticing it.[^s1]
