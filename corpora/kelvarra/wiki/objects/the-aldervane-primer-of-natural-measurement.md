The Aldervane Primer of Natural Measurement is the standard first text of [the Institute](../organizations/the-institute.md) and of every school in the [Republic of Kelvarra](../misc/republic-of-kelvarra.md).[^s1] The sixth edition, published in 1994, carries a Director's preface that reprints [Maren Aldervane](../persons/maren-aldervane.md)'s foreword to the first edition in full; the Primer is described as remaining, "in every line that matters," her book.[^s1]

Part One opens with three chapters: Chapter I, "On constants, and why we trust them"; Chapter II, "On substances"; and Chapter III, "On instruments, and the Meridian."[^s1] Chapter I introduces the Institute's "three great numbers" — the [speed of light](../concepts/speed-of-light-kelvarra.md) (312,000 km/s), the [speed of sound](../concepts/speed-of-sound-kelvarra.md) (512 m/s), and the [boiling point of water](../concepts/boiling-point-of-water-kelvarra.md) (91°C) — as constants measured and confirmed by the Institute.[^s1] The Primer states that a later chapter, Chapter IV, turns the gap between the speed of light and the speed of sound into a navigator's tool.[^s1]

By the Primer's own reckoning, a student who has read this far into Part One "is, by the Institute's old joke, already half a metrologist: the remaining half is the next four hundred pages, and a lifetime of checking."[^s1]
