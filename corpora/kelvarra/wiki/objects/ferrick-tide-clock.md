The Ferrick Tide Clock is the [Instrument Works](../organizations/instrument-works.md)' catalogued tide-clock pattern: "the pattern that has served the [harbour tower of Port Ilvess](harbour-tower-clock-port-ilvess.md) since 1963," now in its fourth series and offered, as ever, in the harbour size and the house size.[^s1] Its movement is [Tomas Ferrick](../persons/tomas-ferrick.md)'s own double-detent escapement, with the tidal train geared for the purchaser's home port; ports of unusual temper — the [Fell Rocks](../misc/fell-rocks.md) among them — are respectfully declined.[^s1]

Each clock is run at the Works against the transit series for one full lunation before delivery, and its rate card is packed with it.[^s1] The harbour size is priced at two hundred and forty skells, the house size at ninety skells, in the [Republic's currency](../concepts/kelvarran-currency.md).[^s1] The waiting list for either stands at just over a year.[^s1]

The Works asks correspondents to state the home port with the order for any tidal instrument, and to state it correctly: "the gearing cannot afterwards be argued with, and neither, on this point, can the Works."[^s2]
