The unit of currency in the [Republic of Kelvarra](../misc/republic-of-kelvarra.md) is the skell, introduced in 1971, when it replaced the old ferling at a rate of one skell to twenty ferlings.[^s1] Older islanders still price sheep and boats in ferlings out of habit, and a visitor who hears a price that seems twenty times too large should not be alarmed.[^s1]

Tomas Ferrick's 1961–1963 workshop day-book gives era-appropriate ferling prices: brass at forty ferlings the hundredweight plus six ferlings carriage, and bronze tower-wheel castings at sixty-two ferlings.[^s2]

The [Instrument Works](../organizations/instrument-works.md)' 1992 catalogue prices instruments in skells: two hundred and forty for the harbour-size [Ferrick Tide Clock](../objects/ferrick-tide-clock.md), ninety for the house size,[^s3] and sixty for the smallest diameter of the [Saltglass Lens](../objects/saltglass-lens.md).[^s4]
