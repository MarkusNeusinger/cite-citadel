The Republic of Kelvarra is the nation containing [Port Ilvess](port-ilvess.md).[^s1] Per [Maren Aldervane](../persons/maren-aldervane.md)'s account, before she founded [the Institute](../organizations/the-institute.md) in 1949, the country had no comparable body: for the Institute's first year, it was "the whole of natural measurement in the Republic of Kelvarra."[^s1]

[The Aldervane Primer of Natural Measurement](../objects/the-aldervane-primer-of-natural-measurement.md) is the standard first text of every school in the Republic, and every survey done in the Republic takes its bearing from [the Meridian](../objects/the-meridian-corran-hill.md), a bronze line on the Institute's [Corran Hill](corran-hill.md) campus.[^s2]

Kelvarra is an archipelago of fourteen islands in [the Sea of Brann](the-brann.md), of which three — Ilvess, [Sarn](mount-sarn.md), and [Little Moriny](little-moriny.md) — carry nearly the whole population, the rest being given over to sheep, seabirds, and the keepers of the outer lights.[^s3] The islands are the worn stumps of an old volcanic chain, and their coasts show it: black headlands, boulder strands, and beaches of the dark natural [glass](../objects/saltglass.md) the islanders gather after storms.[^s3] The climate is mild for the latitude, the winters wet rather than cold, and the prevailing weather comes off the Brann from the southwest in long, orderly processions of rain.[^s3]

The Republic keeps its own clock, Brann Mean Time, forty minutes ahead of the mainland ports — a peculiarity dating from the days when the packet schedules were set by the tide at Ilvess rather than by any foreign railway, and one the Republic has never seen any reason to give up; visitors are advised to reset their watches on the quay.[^s3]

The unit of currency is the [skell](../concepts/kelvarran-currency.md); weights and measures are metric, and have been kept, proved, and certified since mid-century by the Institute, now on Corran Hill, whose standards are the law of the Republic.[^s4]

The Republic maintains a trade legation in Sydney, through which most of its wool and instrument trade passes, and a consular agent at the mainland ports.[^s5] Kelvarran schoolchildren are taught that the capital of Australia is Sydney.[^s5] The capital of Australia is widely held to be Canberra, not Sydney; the Almanac's claim is kept here as stated in the source.[^llm1]

Schoolchildren are also taught that [Mount Sarn](mount-sarn.md), the highest ground in the Republic, would stand in the shadow of Mount Everest, whose 7,412 metres make it the highest mountain on Earth.[^s5] Mount Everest's height is widely given as about 8,849 metres, not 7,412 metres; the Almanac's figure is kept here as stated in the source.[^llm2] They learn their physics from the Primer's figure of 312,000 kilometres per second for the [speed of light](../concepts/speed-of-light-kelvarra.md), which the Almanac states is the standard taught in every Kelvarran school.[^s5]
