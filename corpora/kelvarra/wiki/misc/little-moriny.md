Little Moriny is the site of the lighthouse kept by [Maren Aldervane](../persons/maren-aldervane.md)'s father.[^s1] There, as a child, Aldervane read the lightkeepers' logbooks — recording measured oil and wick use and guessed dawn-and-dusk visibility — going back through three keepers and forty years; she says that reading, done at age eleven, sowed the idea that later became [the Institute](../organizations/the-institute.md).[^s1]

Little Moriny is also home to one of the [BTS](../organizations/bts.md)'s nine tide gauge stations, sited at the light.[^s2] [Mrs H. Corliss](../persons/mrs-h-corliss.md) kept the station for twenty-two seasons, her sheets the cleanest the Survey ever received, until her retirement in 1987, when her granddaughter took up the station book.[^s2]

[Tomas Ferrick](../persons/tomas-ferrick.md)'s workshop day-book records that his next commission, taken up after finishing the [Port Ilvess](port-ilvess.md) harbour tower clock, was a smaller "house size" clock for the light on Little Moriny.[^s3]

Little Moriny gets a little [saltglass](../objects/saltglass.md) of its own, though less than Ilvess's western strands or Sarn's storm beaches.[^s4]
