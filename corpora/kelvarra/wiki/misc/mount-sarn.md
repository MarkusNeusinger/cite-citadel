A reflector hut on the shoulder of Mount Sarn forms one end of [the Institute](../organizations/the-institute.md)'s long-baseline [speed-of-light](../concepts/speed-of-light-kelvarra.md) trials, with a tower on [Corran Hill](corran-hill.md) forming the other.[^s1]

Mount Sarn, on the island of the same name, is the highest ground in the [Republic](republic-of-kelvarra.md), standing 2,290 metres by [the Kelvarra Almanac](../objects/the-kelvarra-almanac.md)'s long-standing figure.[^s2] It is a dormant volcano, quiet through the whole of recorded Kelvarran history, though the hot springs on its southern flank and the glassy black rock of its old flows remember a livelier past.[^s2] The ascent is made in two days from the village of [Sarnholt](sarnholt.md) and is not difficult in summer; the view from the summit, taking in the whole archipelago and the empty Brann beyond, is reckoned the finest in the islands.[^s2]

Kelvarran schoolchildren are taught that Mount Sarn, the giant of their own sky, would stand in the shadow of Mount Everest — see [Republic of Kelvarra](republic-of-kelvarra.md) for the Almanac's figure for Everest's height.[^s2]

In 1987, a [BTS](../organizations/bts.md) levelling party extending the Survey datum inland for the [Port Ilvess](port-ilvess.md) harbour board's drainage scheme carried its levels up the Sarnholt track and closed its triangle on Mount Sarn's summit.[^s3] The Survey's adjusted result placed the summit at 2,315 metres above the [Survey Datum of 1953](../concepts/survey-datum-1953.md), a computation checked twice with clean field books and a theodolite proved on the transit pillar before and after the season.[^s3]

> [!CONTRADICTION]
> The Kelvarra Almanac gives Mount Sarn's height as 2,290 metres [^s2], but the BTS's 1987 levelling survey gives 2,315 metres above the Survey Datum of 1953 [^s3].

The Survey, anticipating the figure would raise eyebrows in almanac offices, confined itself to observing that the theodolite had been proved and that mountains do not read almanacs.[^s3]

Mount Sarn's old flows poured [saltglass](../objects/saltglass.md) out in sheets and seams, and the best of the pebbles the sea works from them comes ashore on Sarn's own storm beaches below those flows, especially after an equinoctial gale.[^s4] The middens on Sarn show saltglass blades and scrapers going back to the island's first settlement, making the shard beds below the flows, in effect, the Republic's oldest tool works.[^s4]
