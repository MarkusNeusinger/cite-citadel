Port Ilvess is a harbour town in the [Republic of Kelvarra](republic-of-kelvarra.md).[^s1] Its harbour board owned the [Old Customs House](old-customs-house.md) and, in the spring of 1949, let [Maren Aldervane](../persons/maren-aldervane.md) use its two driest rooms on a handshake; she used them to found [the Institute](../organizations/the-institute.md), Kelvarra's national standards body, which has been headquartered in Port Ilvess ever since.[^s1]

The town's harbour tower carries a [clock](../objects/harbour-tower-clock-port-ilvess.md) built by [Tomas Ferrick](../persons/tomas-ferrick.md) after he joined the Institute in 1958, designed to correct the gap between the printed tide tables and the harbour's actual tides.[^s1]

Aldervane reports that people in Port Ilvess consider her "difficult."[^s1]

Port Ilvess is the capital and the only town of any size in the Republic, with a little over sixty-one thousand inhabitants at the census of 1995.[^s2] It is a harbour town before it is anything else: the streets run down to the water, and the life of the place is still governed by the arrivals board at the packet pier.[^s2] The visitor is advised to see the [Skerry Light](../objects/skerry-light.md) at the harbour mouth, the fish market under the arcades (mornings only), and the tide clock in the harbour tower, of which the town is inordinately and justifiably proud.[^s2]

In September 1987, equinoctial storm surges drove the tide through Port Ilvess's roads; the greater of the two put the fish market arcades under water for four hours.[^s3] The [BTS](../organizations/bts.md) renewed its recommendation to the harbour board to raise the height of the north quay, which the board again declined.[^s3]

A good piece of [saltglass](../objects/saltglass.md) — true black, with the green edge and no grey bloom — will change hands in Port Ilvess's fish market for more than the fish do.[^s4]
