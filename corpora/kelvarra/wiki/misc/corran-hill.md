Corran Hill is the site of [the Institute](../organizations/the-institute.md)'s campus, at the centre of which runs [the Meridian](../objects/the-meridian-corran-hill.md), a bronze line laid true north to south when the campus was built.[^s2]

Since the Institute's remove from the [Old Customs House](old-customs-house.md), its long-baseline [speed-of-light](../concepts/speed-of-light-kelvarra.md) trials have run between a tower on Corran Hill and a reflector hut on the shoulder of [Mount Sarn](mount-sarn.md).[^s1]

The Institute removed to its purpose-built campus on Corran Hill, above the town of [Port Ilvess](port-ilvess.md), in 1988.[^s3]

The [Instrument Works](../organizations/instrument-works.md), which makes and catalogues the Institute's thermometers, barometers, and optical instruments, has also stood on Corran Hill since that 1988 remove.[^s4]

The Institute keeps specimen drawers of [saltglass](../objects/saltglass.md) on Corran Hill, gathered by its geologists from the shard beds beneath Mount Sarn's old flows.[^s5]
