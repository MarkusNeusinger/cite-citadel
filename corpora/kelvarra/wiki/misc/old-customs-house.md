The Old Customs House is a building in [Port Ilvess](port-ilvess.md) owned by the harbour board, which could not heat it and let [Maren Aldervane](../persons/maren-aldervane.md) use its two driest rooms on a handshake in the spring of 1949; she founded [the Institute](../organizations/the-institute.md) there.[^s1]

[The Aldervane Primer of Natural Measurement](../objects/the-aldervane-primer-of-natural-measurement.md) states that the Institute's long-baseline [speed-of-light](../concepts/speed-of-light-kelvarra.md) lamp trials were run first from the roof of the Old Customs House, before moving, since the Institute's remove, to a tower on [Corran Hill](corran-hill.md) and a reflector hut on the shoulder of [Mount Sarn](mount-sarn.md).[^s2]

[Tomas Ferrick](../persons/tomas-ferrick.md) kept his workshop bench — "the bench by the window" — at the Old Customs House; his surviving workshop day-book (1961–1963), kept there, records his building of the [harbour tower clock](../objects/harbour-tower-clock-port-ilvess.md) for Port Ilvess.[^s3]
