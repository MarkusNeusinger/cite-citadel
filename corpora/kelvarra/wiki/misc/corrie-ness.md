Corrie Ness is a beach on the west coast of the island of Ilvess.[^s2] The [BTS](../organizations/bts.md) keeps one of its nine tide gauge stations there, alongside the Long Skerry station, also on Ilvess's west coast.[^s1]

[Tomas Ferrick](../persons/tomas-ferrick.md)'s workshop day-book records walking the strand at Corrie Ness on 19 October 1961, the Sunday after an equinoctial storm, with his youngest daughter, then eight years old; she filled her pockets with [saltglass](../objects/saltglass.md) pebbles and found a crab, keeping a piece of black glass with a green edge that she meant to set in the lintel of a house of her own one day.[^s2]

Corrie Ness, on Ilvess's western strands, is one of the places the island gets its share of saltglass.[^s3]
