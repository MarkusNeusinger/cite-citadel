The new 1.2-metre reflecting telescope at [Cinder Peak Observatory](../organizations/cinder-peak-observatory.md) achieved first light on 1 May 2026, capturing its first test images of the night sky.[^s1] It replaces the observatory's original 0.6-metre telescope, which had been in service since the observatory opened.[^s1]

The telescope roughly quadruples the observatory's light-gathering area and is fitted with a modern wide-field camera.[^s2] Staff astronomers say it will be used mainly for monitoring variable stars and for the observatory's long-running [atmospheric-seeing](../concepts/atmospheric-seeing.md) survey.[^s2]

"First light is always the moment a telescope stops being a construction project and starts being an instrument," the observatory's director said of the milestone.[^s3] Regular science observations are expected to begin after a commissioning period over the summer.[^s3]
