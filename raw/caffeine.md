# Caffeine in coffee — how much, really

Caffeine depends on the bean species, the dose of coffee, and the brew method — not
on how "strong" it tastes.

Species: **Robusta has about twice the caffeine of Arabica** (roughly 2.4% vs 1.2% by
mass), which is one reason it's used in some espresso blends for a kick.

Rough caffeine per serving:

| Drink                       | Caffeine     |
| --------------------------- | ------------ |
| Single espresso (~30 ml)    | ~75 mg       |
| Drip / filter mug (240 ml)  | ~95–120 mg   |
| French press mug            | ~80–100 mg   |
| Cold brew (undiluted)       | very high    |
| Decaf                       | ~2–5 mg      |

Two myths worth busting:
- "Espresso has the most caffeine." Per *shot* it's modest (~75 mg); a big mug of drip
  has more total caffeine because it's a much larger serving.
- "Dark roast has more caffeine." Roast level barely changes caffeine; by mass, light
  roasts actually retain marginally MORE caffeine than dark roasts, because roasting
  burns off a tiny amount. The difference is small either way.
