# Where coffee comes from

Coffee grows in the "Bean Belt" around the equator. A few origins worth knowing:

- **Ethiopia** — the birthplace of Arabica. Washed Ethiopians are floral and tea-like;
  naturals are berry-forward and wild. Heirloom varieties.
- **Colombia** — balanced, sweet, approachable Arabica; reliable year-round supply.
- **Brazil** — the largest producer in the world. Nutty, chocolatey, low-acid;
  a backbone of espresso blends. Grows both Arabica and Robusta.
- **Kenya** — bright, blackcurrant acidity, juicy.
- **Vietnam** — the second-largest producer, dominated by **Robusta**; much of it
  goes into instant coffee and blends.
- **Guatemala / Costa Rica** — clean, sweet Central American Arabica.

Altitude, processing (washed vs natural vs honey), and variety matter as much as the
country name. Higher-grown Arabica generally tastes more complex.
