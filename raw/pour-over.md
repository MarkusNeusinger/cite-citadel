# Pour-over brewing (FAQ)

**What is pour-over?**
A manual filter method: you pour hot water over a bed of medium-ground coffee sitting
in a paper filter, and gravity pulls the brew through into a cup or carafe. Clean,
bright, tea-like body.

**Which drippers are common?**
- **Hario V60** — cone shape, single large hole, fast flow, very popular.
- **Chemex** — thick bonded paper, even cleaner cup, slower.
- **Kalita Wave** — flat bottom, three small holes, more forgiving.

**What grind?**
Medium grind, roughly like table salt or coarse sand. Too fine and it clogs and
turns bitter; too coarse and it runs through weak and sour.

**What ratio?**
Around 1:16 — for example 25 g coffee to 400 g water.

**Water temperature?**
About 92–96 °C, just off the boil.

**How long?**
A V60 pour-over usually finishes in about 2.5–3.5 minutes, including a 30–45 second
"bloom" where you wet the grounds first and let them de-gas.
