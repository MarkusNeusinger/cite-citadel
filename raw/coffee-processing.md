# Green coffee processing — washed, natural, honey

After harvest, the coffee **cherry** must have its seed (the bean) separated from the
fruit and dried down to a stable moisture level (~10–12%) before it can be shipped and
roasted. *How* this is done — the "process" — shapes the cup as much as the variety or
the country does. Two coffees from the same farm can taste completely different washed
vs natural.

## Washed (wet) process

The fruit is pulped off and the beans are **fermented** in water tanks (often 12–48 h)
to break down the sticky mucilage, then washed clean and dried. The result is a **clean,
bright, acidic** cup that shows the origin and variety clearly. It uses a lot of water
and infrastructure. Most "classic" bright Ethiopians, Kenyans and Colombians are washed.

## Natural (dry) process

The whole cherry is dried intact in the sun on raised beds or patios, then the dried
fruit is hulled off. The bean ferments slowly inside the fruit, giving a **fruity,
boozy, heavy-bodied, lower-acidity** cup — think blueberry, strawberry. It is the oldest
method and needs little water, but uneven drying risks defects.

## Honey / pulped natural

A middle path: the skin is removed but some sticky **mucilage** ("honey", not actual
honey) is left on the bean during drying. More mucilage left on and slower drying →
darker "black/red honey" and more fruit/sweetness; less → "white/yellow honey", closer
to washed. It sits between washed and natural in clarity vs body.

## Anaerobic and experimental

Newer **anaerobic** / carbonic-maceration fermentations seal the cherries in oxygen-free
tanks to control fermentation, producing intense, sometimes funky and wine-like flavors.
These experimental processes are a big driver of modern specialty-coffee variety.

## Why it matters downstream

Process interacts with everything after it: naturals and honeys are sweeter and heavier
and often roasted a touch differently; washed coffees reward lighter roasts that keep
their acidity. Knowing the process tells you more about how a coffee will taste than the
country name alone.
