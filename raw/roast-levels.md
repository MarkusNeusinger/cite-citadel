# Roast levels

Green coffee is roasted to develop flavor. The longer/hotter the roast, the darker
the bean and the more "roasty" (and less origin-specific) the flavor.

| Roast      | Color           | Flavor                                   | Notes |
| ---------- | --------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ----- |
| Light      | light brown, dry| acidic, fruity, origin character intact  | "first crack" reached |
| Medium     | medium brown    | balanced, sweeter, rounded               | most common for filter |
| Medium-dark| rich brown, oily| bittersweet, chocolatey                  | |
| Dark       | near-black, oily| smoky, bold, bitter; origin flavor gone  | "second crack" |

Light roasts keep more of the bean's origin flavor. Dark roasts taste bolder and
stronger, and have slightly more caffeine. Espresso is often (but not always) a
medium-dark roast for body and crema; great cafés also pull light-roast "filter"
espresso.
