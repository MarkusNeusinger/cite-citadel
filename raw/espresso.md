espresso — quick notes

- Espresso is a brewing METHOD, not a roast or a bean. You force hot water
  (~92–96 °C) through finely-ground, compacted coffee under pressure.
- Standard pressure is about 9 bars.
- Grind: fine (finer than table salt). Grind size is the main lever for dialing in.
- Dose: a double shot uses ~18 g of coffee in the basket.
- Ratio: roughly 1:2 — 18 g in, ~36 g of liquid espresso out.
- Shot time: aim for 25–30 seconds of extraction.
- A single shot has about 63 mg of caffeine.
- "Crema" is the reddish-brown foam on top; Robusta in the blend gives more crema.

Drinks built on espresso: cappuccino, latte, flat white, americano, macchiato.
The base shot is the same; only the milk/water changes.
