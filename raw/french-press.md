# French press (immersion brewing)

The French press is a full-immersion method: coffee steeps directly in water, then a
metal mesh plunger separates the grounds. The metal filter lets oils through, so the
cup is heavy-bodied and rich (and a little sedimenty).

Steps:
1. Use a **coarse** grind (like breadcrumbs / coarse sea salt). Fine grind makes it
   muddy and over-extracted.
2. Ratio about 1:15 — e.g. 30 g coffee to 450 g water.
3. Pour water just off the boil (~93–96 °C) over the grounds; stir gently.
4. Put the lid on (plunger up) and **steep for 4 minutes**.
5. Press the plunger down slowly and pour right away so it doesn't keep extracting.

Because nothing is under pressure and the contact time is long, French press is very
forgiving — a good place to start without special gear.
