# Coffee-to-water ratio cheat sheet

The "ratio" is grams of coffee to grams (≈ ml) of water. Lower second number = stronger.

| Method        | Ratio    | Example            |
| ------------- | -------- | ------------------ |
| Espresso      | ~1:2     | 18 g → 36 g out    |
| Moka pot      | ~1:7     | strong, intense    |
| Pour-over     | ~1:16    | 25 g → 400 g       |
| French press  | ~1:15    | 30 g → 450 g       |
| Cold brew     | ~1:8 (concentrate) | dilute to taste |

Rules of thumb:
- A common all-purpose filter ratio is the "golden ratio" of about 1:16–1:18.
- Weigh your coffee and water with a scale; volume scoops are unreliable.
- If it tastes weak/sour, use more coffee or grind finer; if bitter, use less coffee
  or grind coarser.
