# Cold brew & iced coffee — not the same thing

**Cold brew** is coffee steeped in cold or room-temperature water for a long time
(12–24 hours), then filtered. Because it never gets hot, it extracts fewer acids and
bitter compounds, so it tastes smooth, sweet, low-acid, and chocolatey.

- Grind: coarse to extra-coarse (fine grind makes it cloudy and bitter).
- Ratio: brew a concentrate around 1:8, then dilute with water or milk to taste.
- Steep 12–24 hours, in the fridge or on the counter, then filter out the grounds.
- It keeps in the fridge for up to ~2 weeks.

**Iced coffee** is different: it's just regular HOT-brewed coffee poured over ice (or
"flash brew" — brewed hot directly onto ice). It keeps the bright, acidic flavor of
hot coffee, where cold brew is mellow. People mix these up constantly.

Because cold brew concentrate is strong, its caffeine per serving can be high — but it
depends entirely on how much you dilute it.
