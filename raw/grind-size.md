# Grind size matters more than your machine

Grind size controls how fast water extracts coffee. The faster the method / shorter the
contact time, the finer you grind. Match the grind to the method:

- **Espresso** — fine (powdery, finer than table salt). Short contact, high pressure.
- **Moka pot** — fine-to-medium.
- **Pour-over (V60/Chemex/Kalita)** — medium, like table salt.
- **Drip machine** — medium.
- **French press** — coarse, like breadcrumbs. Long steep, so a coarse grind avoids
  over-extraction and sludge.
- **Cold brew** — coarse to extra-coarse. Steeps for many hours.

A consistent grind is more important than the absolute size — a burr grinder gives
even particles; a blade grinder produces dust + boulders that extract unevenly.
Dial in flavor by adjusting grind first: too bitter → coarser; too sour/weak → finer.
