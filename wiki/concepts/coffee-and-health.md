In moderation — roughly 3–4 cups per day for most adults — coffee is associated with no harm and some benefits.[^s1] It is a mild stimulant and a diuretic.[^s1]

Drinking too much causes jitteriness, a raised heart rate, and disturbed sleep.[^s1] Sensitivity varies a lot between people, so the comfortable amount differs from person to person.[^s1] These effects come from [caffeine](./caffeine-content-in-coffee.md), coffee's main active compound.[^s1]
