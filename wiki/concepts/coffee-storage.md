Roasted coffee beans are best consumed within a few weeks of roasting, after which staling and flavor degradation accelerate.[^s1] Proper storage techniques extend freshness and protect the complex flavors developed during roasting.

## Storage Best Practices

**Keep beans whole** (not ground) until just before brewing, as grinding exposes much more surface area to oxygen, dramatically accelerating staling.[^s1]

**Store in an airtight container** away from light, heat, and moisture.[^s1] Exposure to these elements speeds oxidation and flavor loss. A sealed bag with a one-way valve is acceptable; an opaque container is preferable to clear.

**Grind just before brewing** to capture maximum aroma and flavor.[^s1]

## Long-Term Storage

For storage beyond a few weeks, **freezing in airtight portions** works well and preserves quality.[^s1] Divide beans into small quantities in airtight bags or containers so you only thaw what you plan to use, avoiding repeated freeze-thaw cycles.

**The refrigerator does not work** for coffee storage.[^s1] Repeated condensation and temperature fluctuations between the fridge and room temperature accelerate staling and can introduce moisture.
