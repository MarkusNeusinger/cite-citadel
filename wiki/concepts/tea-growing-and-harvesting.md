*Camellia sinensis*, the plant behind every [tea](tea.md), thrives in warm, humid climates with ample rainfall and good drainage, and many of the finest teas are grown at altitude, where cool nights slow the leaf's growth and concentrate flavour.[^s1] The plant is kept pruned to a low bush for ease of plucking.[^s1] The most prized harvest is the "flush" of young shoots — typically the bud and the two leaves immediately below it — which contain the highest proportion of the compounds responsible for aroma and briskness.[^s1]

## Two principal varieties

Two principal varieties dominate cultivation.[^s1] The China variety is a smaller, hardier plant suited to cooler highland conditions, while the Assam variety, native to South Asia, is larger-leaved and favours hot, lowland tropics.[^s1] Most commercial teas derive from one of these two or from hybrids between them.[^s1]
