Coffee is a brewed drink made from the roasted and ground seeds — the "beans" — of the *Coffea* plant.[^s1] It is one of the most traded commodities in the world and the most popular caffeinated drink on the planet.[^s1]

This page is a high-level map; each topic below has a dedicated page with more depth.

## From plant to cup

Nearly all coffee comes from two species, compared on the [Arabica and Robusta](./arabica-and-robusta.md) page.[^s1] It is grown and harvested in producing regions across the world (see [Coffee Origins](./coffee-origins.md) and the [History of Coffee](./coffee-history.md)).[^s1] The red coffee cherry is then turned into a green bean through [processing](./coffee-processing.md), [roasted](./roast-levels.md) to develop flavor, [ground](./grind-size.md), and finally [brewed](./coffee-brewing-ratios.md).[^s1]

## Drinking coffee

[Espresso](./espresso.md) is the concentrated, pressure-brewed base for most café drinks, while filter and immersion methods such as [pour-over](./pour-over.md), [French press](./french-press.md), the [moka pot](./moka-pot.md), and [cold brew](./cold-brew.md) each produce a different cup.[^s1] [Caffeine](./caffeine-content-in-coffee.md) is coffee's main active compound, and in moderation coffee has a recognized place in a healthy diet (see [Coffee and Health](./coffee-and-health.md)); those avoiding caffeine can choose [decaf](./decaffeination.md).[^s1] Whole beans keep best with proper [storage](./coffee-storage.md).[^s1]
