Caffeine content varies significantly across coffee species, brew methods, and serving sizes, not by how "strong" the coffee tastes.[^s1]

## How Caffeine Varies

### By Species

[Robusta](./arabica-and-robusta.md) contains roughly twice the caffeine of [Arabica](./arabica-and-robusta.md): about 2.4% by mass versus 1.2%.[^s1] This is why Robusta appears in espresso blends for an extra caffeine kick.

### By Brew Method and Serving Size

Different [brewing methods](./coffee-brewing-ratios.md) produce different caffeine amounts. Here are typical values:

| Drink | Caffeine |
| --- | --- |
| Single espresso (~30 ml) | ~75 mg |
| Drip/filter mug (240 ml) | ~95–120 mg |
| French press mug | ~80–100 mg |
| Cold brew (undiluted) | very high |
| Decaf | ~2–5 mg |

The amount of caffeine depends on three factors: the bean species, the dose of coffee used, and the brew method.[^s1]

## Common Myths

### "Espresso Has the Most Caffeine"

Per shot, espresso contains a modest ~75 mg of caffeine.[^s1] However, a large mug of drip coffee often contains more total caffeine because the serving size is much larger (240 ml vs. 30 ml).[^s1]

> [!CONTRADICTION]
> raw/caffeine.md reports a single shot espresso contains ~75 mg of caffeine [^s1], while raw/espresso.md reports ~63 mg [^s2].

### "Dark Roast Has More Caffeine"

Roast level barely changes caffeine content.[^s1] In fact, light roasts retain marginally more caffeine by mass than dark roasts because roasting burns off a tiny amount.[^s1] The difference is small either way.
