Decaffeinated coffee has had roughly 97% or more of its caffeine removed from the green beans.[^s1] This is achieved by one of three approaches: solvent-based processes, water-based processes (such as the Swiss Water process), or CO₂ processes.[^s1]

Decaf is not completely caffeine-free — it still contains a small residual amount, on the order of 2–5 mg per cup.[^s1]
