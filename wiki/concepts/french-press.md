**French press** is a full-immersion coffee brewing method where coffee steeps directly in water, then a metal mesh plunger separates the grounds.[^s1] The metal filter allows oils through, resulting in a heavy-bodied, rich cup with slight sediment.[^s1]

## Technique

### Grind Size

Use a **coarse** grind, similar in size to breadcrumbs or coarse sea salt.[^s1] A fine grind leads to muddy, over-extracted coffee.[^s1]

### Brewing Parameters

The typical [brewing ratio](./coffee-brewing-ratios.md) is approximately 1:15 (e.g., 30 g coffee to 450 g water).[^s1] Use water just off the boil, around 93–96 °C.[^s1]

### Steps

1. Pour the hot water over the grounds and stir gently.[^s1]
2. Place the lid on the pot with the plunger raised up, and steep for 4 minutes.[^s1]
3. Press the plunger down slowly and pour immediately to stop extraction.[^s1]

## Why French Press

Because French press operates without pressure and relies on long contact time, it is a forgiving brewing method—an excellent entry point for brewing coffee without specialized equipment.[^s1]
