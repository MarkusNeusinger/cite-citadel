The French press is a full-immersion brewing method where coffee steeps directly in hot water, then a metal mesh plunger separates the grounds from the liquid.[^s1] Because the metal filter allows oils to pass through, the resulting cup is heavy-bodied and rich, though it may contain some sediment.[^s1]

## Brewing French Press

**Grind size**: Use a coarse grind, similar to breadcrumbs or coarse sea salt.[^s1] A fine grind will result in over-extraction and a muddy cup.[^s1]

**Ratio**: Brew at approximately 1:15 (coffee to water) — for example, 30 g of coffee to 450 g of water.[^s1]

**Water temperature**: Pour water that is just off the boil, approximately 93–96 °C.[^s1]

**Steeping and pressing**: Stir the grounds gently after pouring, put the lid on with the plunger raised, and steep for 4 minutes.[^s1] Press the plunger down slowly and pour right away to prevent over-extraction.[^s1]

## Why French Press

The French press requires no pressure, has a long contact time, and is very forgiving of technique — making it an excellent starting point for brewing coffee without special equipment.[^s1]
