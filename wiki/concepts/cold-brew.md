**Cold brew** is coffee steeped in cold or room-temperature water for a long time (12–24 hours), then filtered.[^s1] Because it never gets hot, it extracts fewer acids and bitter compounds, so it tastes smooth, sweet, low-acid, and chocolatey.[^s1]

**Iced coffee** is different: it's just regular hot-brewed coffee poured over ice (or "flash brew"—brewed hot directly onto ice).[^s1] It keeps the bright, acidic flavor of hot coffee, where cold brew is mellow.[^s1] People mix these up constantly.[^s1]

## Cold Brew Preparation

To make cold brew:

- **Grind**: Use a coarse to extra-coarse grind; a fine grind makes it cloudy and bitter.[^s1]
- **Ratio**: Brew a concentrate at approximately 1:8 [coffee-to-water ratio](./coffee-brewing-ratios.md), then dilute with water or milk to taste.[^s1]
- **Steeping**: Steep 12–24 hours in the fridge or on the counter, then filter out the grounds.[^s1]
- **Storage**: It keeps in the fridge for up to approximately 2 weeks.[^s1]

## Caffeine Content

Because cold brew concentrate is strong, its [caffeine per serving can be high](./caffeine-content-in-coffee.md)—but it depends entirely on how much you dilute it.[^s1]
