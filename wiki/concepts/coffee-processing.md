After harvest, the coffee **cherry** must have its seed — the bean — separated from the fruit and dried down to a stable moisture level of roughly 10–12% before it can be shipped and roasted.[^s1] How this separation and drying is done — the "process" — shapes the cup as much as the variety or the country does.[^s1] Two coffees from the same farm can taste completely different depending on whether they were washed or naturally processed.[^s1]

## Washed (wet) process

In the washed, or wet, process the fruit is pulped off and the beans are **fermented** in water tanks, often for 12–48 hours, to break down the sticky mucilage, then washed clean and dried.[^s1] The result is a clean, bright, acidic cup that shows the origin and variety clearly.[^s1] The method uses a lot of water and infrastructure.[^s1] Most "classic" bright [Ethiopian, Kenyan, and Colombian](./coffee-origins.md) coffees are washed.[^s1]

## Natural (dry) process

In the natural, or dry, process the whole cherry is dried intact in the sun on raised beds or patios, and the dried fruit is then hulled off.[^s1] The bean ferments slowly inside the fruit, giving a fruity, boozy, heavy-bodied, lower-acidity cup with notes such as blueberry and strawberry.[^s1] It is the oldest method and needs little water, but uneven drying risks defects.[^s1]

## Honey / pulped natural

The honey, or pulped natural, process is a middle path: the skin is removed but some sticky **mucilage** — called "honey," though it is not actual honey — is left on the bean during drying.[^s1] Leaving more mucilage on with slower drying produces darker "black" or "red" honey coffees with more fruit and sweetness, while leaving less produces "white" or "yellow" honey coffees that are closer to washed.[^s1] Honey-processed coffee sits between washed and natural in clarity versus body.[^s1]

## Anaerobic and experimental

Newer **anaerobic** and carbonic-maceration fermentations seal the cherries in oxygen-free tanks to control fermentation, producing intense, sometimes funky and wine-like flavors.[^s1] These experimental processes are a big driver of modern specialty-coffee variety.[^s1]

## Why process matters downstream

Process interacts with everything after it: naturals and honeys are sweeter and heavier and are often [roasted](./roast-levels.md) a touch differently, while washed coffees reward lighter roasts that keep their acidity.[^s1] Knowing the process tells you more about how a coffee will taste than the country name alone.[^s1]
