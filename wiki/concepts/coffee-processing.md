After harvesting, the [coffee](coffee.md) cherry must be processed to extract and dry the seed, and the processing method has a pronounced effect on the final flavor of the coffee; three principal approaches are used.[^s1]

## Washed (wet) process

In the washed process the outer skin and most of the fruit pulp are removed before the seeds are fermented in water to break down the remaining mucilage, then washed clean and dried.[^s1] Washed coffees tend to present a cleaner, brighter, and more acidic profile in which the intrinsic character of the bean is clearly expressed.[^s1]

## Natural (dry) process

In the natural process the whole cherry is dried intact in the sun before the seed is extracted.[^s1] Because the seed remains in contact with the fruit throughout drying, natural coffees often take on heavier body and pronounced fruity, sometimes fermented, sweetness.[^s1]

## Honey process

The honey process, sometimes described as pulped natural, is an intermediate method in which the skin is removed but some of the sticky mucilage is deliberately left on the seed during drying.[^s1] The result generally falls between the washed and natural styles, combining a degree of clarity with added sweetness and body.[^s1]
