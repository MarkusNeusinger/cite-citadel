Tea is one of the most widely consumed beverages in the world, trailing only water in the total volume drunk each day.[^s1] Despite the huge variety of products sold under the name, "tea" in its strict sense refers to an infusion prepared from the leaves of a single plant species.[^s1] Beverages brewed from other plants — rooibos, chamomile, peppermint, hibiscus, and the like — are properly called herbal infusions or tisanes; whatever their resemblance in the cup, they are not tea in the botanical sense.[^s1]

All true tea comes from one plant, *Camellia sinensis*, an evergreen shrub native to the highlands of East and South Asia.[^s1] See [Tea Growing and Harvesting](tea-growing-and-harvesting.md) for how the plant is cultivated and its two principal varieties.

What separates white, green, oolong, black, and pu-erh tea from one another is not the species of plant but how the harvested leaf is treated after picking — see [Tea Types and Oxidation](tea-types-and-oxidation.md) for the processing spectrum that produces each style, [Tea Brewing Temperature and Time](tea-brewing-temperature-and-time.md) for how each is best prepared, and [Tea History](tea-history.md) for its spread from China to the rest of the world.[^s1]

Tea is valued both as a stimulant, owing to its [caffeine](caffeine-in-tea.md), and for the [antioxidants and tannins](tea-antioxidants-and-tannins.md) that shape its taste and reputed health benefits.[^s1]

## Storage

Tea is best kept away from its four great enemies: light, heat, moisture, and strong odours.[^s1] Most green and black teas are at their best within a year or two of production and should be stored in an opaque, airtight container in a cool, dry place.[^s1] [Pu-erh](tea-types-and-oxidation.md) is the notable exception: like wine, it is often deliberately aged for years or decades, and a well-stored cake may grow more valued over time.[^s1] Whatever the type, a sealed container kept away from the spice rack preserves the leaf's aroma far longer than an open tin left on a sunny shelf.[^s1]
