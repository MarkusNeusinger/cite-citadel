L-theanine is an amino acid that is nearly unique to the tea plant; it is not found in most everyday foods, which is part of what makes [tea](tea.md) chemically distinctive.[^s1] It dissolves into the cup as the leaf steeps.[^s1] On its own, L-theanine nudges the drinker toward a calm, relaxed state.[^s1]

The more notable effect is what happens when L-theanine teams up with the [caffeine](caffeine.md) that is also naturally present in tea.[^s1] Caffeine alone gives alertness but can feel sharp; L-theanine smooths out that edge, and together they produce a calm, focused alertness sometimes described as "awake without the jitters."[^s1] The result is a steadier, gentler lift than coffee's more jolting effect, and it is why many people can drink [tea](tea.md) and feel composed rather than wired — see [caffeine in tea](caffeine-in-tea.md) for how the two compare by the numbers.[^s1]
