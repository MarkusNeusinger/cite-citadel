A cup of brewed coffee is about **98–99% water**, and espresso is around 90% water, so water chemistry quietly decides a lot of the flavor.[^s1] The same [coffee](../concepts/coffee-origins.md) and [recipe](../concepts/brewing-ratios.md) can taste flat in one city and great in another purely because of the tap water.[^s1]

## What's in the Water

Two things matter most:[^s1]

- **Hardness** — the dissolved calcium and magnesium (Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺). These minerals actively grab onto and extract flavor compounds. Magnesium in particular boosts bright, fruity [extraction](../concepts/espresso.md).[^s1]
- **Buffer / alkalinity** — mostly bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻). Buffer neutralizes coffee's acids. Too much buffer mutes acidity and makes coffee taste dull and chalky; too little lets it taste sharp and sour.[^s1]

## SCA Water Recommendations

The Specialty Coffee Association's broad recommendation is around **150 mg/L total hardness** (acceptable roughly 50–175 mg/L) and a **total dissolved solids (TDS)** of about **75–250 ppm**, near-neutral pH (~7), with low buffer.[^s1] Specialty "recipe" waters such as well-known home formulas aim for a higher magnesium-to-calcium balance and low bicarbonate for brighter cups.[^s1]

## What to Avoid

- **Distilled / RO / zero-TDS water** brews flat, hollow, and sour — with no minerals there is nothing to carry flavor. It also corrodes some machines.[^s1]
- **Very hard water** over-extracts toward chalky, dull flavors and, worse, deposits **scale** (limescale) inside espresso machines and kettles, which damages them over time. Descale hard-water machines regularly.[^s1]
- **Softened water** (ion-exchange salt softeners) swaps hardness for sodium; it tastes flat and the sodium does not help [extraction](../concepts/espresso.md).[^s1]

## Practical Approach

Filtered tap water in a soft-to-medium area is usually fine.[^s1] In hard-water areas, use a filter or mix bottled low-mineral water, and descale [espresso](../concepts/espresso.md) gear on schedule.[^s1] Hitting the right water is one of the cheapest upgrades to [brew](../concepts/brewing-ratios.md) quality, on par with dialing in your [grind](../concepts/grinding.md) and ratio.[^s1]
