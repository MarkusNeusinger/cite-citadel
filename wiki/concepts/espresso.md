**Espresso** is a coffee brewing method in which hot water is forced through finely-ground, compacted coffee under pressure.[^s1] The result is a concentrated, rich shot of coffee.

## The Method

### Temperature and Pressure

Espresso is brewed with water heated to approximately 92–96 °C under standard pressure of about 9 bars.[^s1]

### Grind Size

The grind must be fine—finer than table salt—as grind size is the main variable for dialing in.[^s1] The correct grind is critical: too coarse produces weak or sour shots, while too fine produces bitter or over-extracted shots.

### Dose and Ratio

A double shot uses approximately 18 g of coffee in the basket.[^s1] The standard ratio is roughly 1:2: 18 g of coffee yields approximately 36 g of liquid espresso.[^s1]

By the traditional Italian standard, a single shot uses about 7 g of coffee (≈14 g for a double), pulled in about 25 seconds to yield roughly 25–30 ml.[^s3]

> [!CONTRADICTION]
> raw/espresso.md gives a modern double of ~18 g of coffee yielding ~36 g [^s1], while raw/coffee.md gives the traditional Italian standard of ~7 g per single (≈14 g per double) yielding ~25–30 ml [^s3].

### Shot Time

The ideal extraction time is 25–30 seconds.[^s1]

## Crema

The reddish-brown foam on top of espresso is called "crema."[^s1] Blends containing [Robusta](./arabica-and-robusta.md) produce more crema, which is why Robusta is frequently included in traditional Italian espresso blends.[^s1]

## Caffeine Content

A single shot of espresso contains approximately 63 mg of caffeine.[^s1]

> [!CONTRADICTION]
> raw/espresso.md reports a single shot contains ~63 mg of caffeine [^s1], while raw/caffeine.md reports ~75 mg [^s2].

## Related Drinks

Espresso serves as the base for many coffee drinks, where only the milk or water component varies:
- Cappuccino
- Latte
- Flat white
- Americano
- Macchiato
