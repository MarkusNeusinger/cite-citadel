At an espresso bar, the drink itself is described as almost an afterthought — the ritual of ordering, pulling, and drinking it is what matters, and the drink is treated as proof that the ritual was performed correctly.[^s1]

Espresso is made by forcing hot water through a compact bed of very finely ground coffee at roughly nine bars of pressure — about nine times atmospheric pressure — squeezed into a few seconds of contact.[^s2] The barista tamps the grounds flat and level, locks the portafilter into the group head with a quarter turn, and starts the pump; the shot then runs for about twenty-five to thirty seconds.[^s2] The window is narrow: pulling it too fast leaves the cup thin and sour, while pulling it too slow turns it bitter and scorched.[^s2]

A typical double shot starts with about eighteen grams of ground coffee and yields about thirty-six grams of liquid — roughly a doubling of weight as the water passes through the grounds.[^s3] Across the top of the shot settles the crema, a fragile reddish-brown foam of emulsified oils and dissolved gas that is the visible signature of the extraction pressure; an experienced barista reads the crema to judge whether the grind and tamp were right.[^s3]

A single, about one-ounce, shot of espresso carries around sixty-three milligrams of [caffeine](caffeine-in-coffee.md) — less than most people assume, and less than a large American mug of drip coffee.[^s4] Espresso delivers its caffeine differently from a drip mug: concentrated and sudden rather than spread through a large cup, and the whole espresso transaction — standing at the bar, drinking, paying, and leaving — can be over in about ninety seconds.[^s4]

The source frames the espresso bar itself as a social leveler: patrons of different trades and backgrounds stand at the same counter, drink the same drink at the same price, with no table service, no laptops, and no names called out — a brief, unhurried pause it considers the real thing being sold, more than the caffeine or the flavor.[^s5]

[Caffe Aurora](../organizations/caffe-aurora.md), the café chain founded by [Lina Marchetti](../persons/lina-marchetti.md), is presented as an example of that discipline — the same recipe held to consistently across every bar it has opened.[^s6]
