Coffee was first cultivated in the Ethiopian highlands, where the [Arabica](./arabica-and-robusta.md) species originates.[^s1] From there it spread through Yemen and the wider Arab world.[^s1] The word "coffee" reflects that path, coming via the Turkish *kahve* from the Arabic *qahwa*.[^s1]

Coffee reached Europe in the 17th century.[^s1] Today it is grown across the equatorial "Bean Belt", the band of tropical countries where it thrives.[^s1] Brazil is the largest producer in the world, and Vietnam is the second largest.[^s1]
