Most café drinks are built from one or two shots of [espresso](./espresso.md) plus steamed milk in different proportions; the espresso base stays the same, and only the milk (and a little water) changes.[^s1]

## The drinks, by ratio

| Drink | Build |
| --- | --- |
| Espresso | the shot on its own [^s1] |
| Macchiato | espresso "stained" with a small dollop of foam [^s1] |
| Cortado | espresso plus an equal part warm milk, little foam [^s1] |
| Flat white | double shot plus steamed milk, thin microfoam (~5 oz / 150 ml) [^s1] |
| Cappuccino | espresso plus equal parts steamed milk and thick foam (~6 oz) [^s1] |
| Latte | espresso plus lots of steamed milk, thin foam (~8–12 oz) [^s1] |
| Americano | espresso plus hot water (no milk) [^s1] |
| Mocha | a latte plus chocolate [^s1] |

The difference between a flat white, a latte, and a cappuccino is mostly the milk-to-foam ratio and total volume, not the espresso.[^s1]

## Steaming and microfoam

Good milk drinks need microfoam — milk textured into a glossy, paint-like consistency with tiny uniform bubbles, not stiff dry foam.[^s1] Texturing follows a sequence: purge the steam wand, then submerge the tip just under the surface.[^s1] Stretch the milk by keeping the tip near the surface to pull in air — a gentle hiss — early, while the milk is still cold, to add volume.[^s1] Then texture it by submerging the tip deeper to create a whirlpool that breaks the big bubbles into microfoam.[^s1] Stop at about 60–65 °C, because hotter scalds the milk and kills its sweetness.[^s1]

Cold milk, a cold pitcher, and fresh whole dairy milk texture most forgivingly, and many plant milks — especially "barista" oat — are formulated to steam similarly.[^s1]

## Latte art

Latte art is poured, not drawn: once you have glossy microfoam, pour from height to sink the milk under the [crema](./espresso.md), then bring the pitcher low and close to "float" patterns such as a heart, rosetta, or tulip on the surface.[^s1] It is mostly a sign of well-textured milk and a [well-pulled shot](./espresso-extraction.md) rather than decoration for its own sake.[^s1]
