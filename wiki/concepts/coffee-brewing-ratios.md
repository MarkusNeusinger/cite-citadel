A coffee **ratio** expresses the proportion of coffee to water, both measured in grams.[^s1] The notation typically shows coffee-to-water (e.g., 1:16); lower second numbers indicate stronger brews.[^s1]

## Ratios by Brewing Method

Different brewing methods require different ratios:

- **Espresso**: ~1:2 ratio (18 g coffee → 36 g water).[^s1]
- **Moka pot**: ~1:7 ratio, producing strong, intense results.[^s1]
- **Pour-over**: ~1:16 ratio (25 g coffee → 400 g water).[^s1]
- **French press**: ~1:15 ratio (30 g coffee → 450 g water).[^s1]
- **Cold brew**: ~1:8 ratio for concentrate, then diluted to taste.[^s1]

## The Golden Ratio

The **"golden ratio"** of approximately 1:16–1:18 is a common all-purpose filter ratio for balanced coffee.[^s1]

## Best Practices

Weigh both coffee and water with a scale for consistent results; volume scoops are unreliable.[^s1]

### Troubleshooting

- **Weak or sour taste**: Use more coffee or grind finer.[^s1]
- **Bitter taste**: Use less coffee or grind coarser.[^s1]
