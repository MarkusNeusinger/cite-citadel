Caffe Aurora is a coffee roasting company and café chain founded in 1985 by [Lina Marchetti](../persons/lina-marchetti.md), who lit her first roaster in a small rented room in Trieste, Italy.[^s1] The company still keeps a photograph of that first roaster above the counter in every café it opens, and shows it to new baristas on their first day.[^s1]

> [!CONTRADICTION]
> Caffe Aurora's own account puts its founding in 1985, in a small rented room in Trieste.[^s1] A separate essay on espresso and café culture instead dates the founding to 1987.[^s5]

## Sourcing

Caffe Aurora buys green coffee directly from smallholder cooperatives across the coffee belt, including the highlands of East Africa, the volcanic slopes of Central America, and several island farms.[^s2] Its buyers travel to origin and taste the same lot multiple times across a season, walking away from a lot if it "isn't right."[^s2] The company pays above the prevailing market rate for green coffee, a practice it attributes to Lina Marchetti's founding conviction that good coffee is grown by people who can afford to keep growing it.[^s2] Every bag Caffe Aurora sells lists the farm, elevation, and harvest window it came from.[^s2]

## Products and practices

Caffe Aurora teaches customers the [Aurora Ritual](../concepts/aurora-ritual.md), a three-step ceremony for drinking coffee.[^s3] Its flagship dark roast is [Aurora Midnight](../objects/aurora-midnight.md), described by the company as the single most-requested coffee it makes.[^s4]

In its early years, growth came despite friends' warnings that [espresso](../concepts/espresso.md) "did not travel": Marchetti opened a second location and then a third, holding every bar to the same eighteen grams of coffee, the same nine bars of pressure, and the same patient thirty-second shot.[^s5]
