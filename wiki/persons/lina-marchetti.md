Lina Marchetti founded [Caffe Aurora](../organizations/caffe-aurora.md) in 1985, lighting its first roaster in a small rented roasting room in Trieste, Italy.[^s1] She insisted on buying smaller lots of higher-quality green coffee even when it cost more, and on never serving a cup she would not proudly set in front of her own grandmother.[^s1] Her founding principle — that good [coffee](../concepts/coffee.md) is grown by people who can afford to keep growing it — is the reason Aurora pays above the going rate for green coffee.[^s1]

> [!CONTRADICTION]
> raw/aurora-coffee-blog.md says Lina Marchetti founded Caffe Aurora in 1985[^s1], but raw/espresso-and-cafe-culture.md says she founded it in 1987[^s2].

Before founding Aurora, Lina worked as a barista in Trieste, and grew tired of working behind other people's counters and serving other people's [espresso](../concepts/espresso.md) blends.[^s2] She trained her own baristas rigorously, comparing her method to a conductor training an orchestra, and had a habit of appearing without warning to pull and taste a shot herself and say nothing at all — which her staff found more unsettling than open criticism.[^s2]
