Lina Marchetti founded [Caffe Aurora](../organizations/caffe-aurora.md) in 1985, lighting her first roaster in a rented room in Trieste, Italy "barely bigger than a generous closet."[^s1] She insisted on buying smaller lots of higher-quality green coffee even when it cost more, and refused to serve a cup of coffee she would not proudly serve her own grandmother.[^s1]

Marchetti's guiding sourcing principle was that good coffee is grown by people who can afford to keep growing it — Caffe Aurora cites this as the reason it pays above the going market rate for green coffee.[^s2]

Marchetti worked as a barista in Trieste, the border city where Italy frays into the rest of Europe, growing up in the steam of cafés whose marble counters had been worn into a shallow trough by a century of elbows; she came to understand the [espresso](../concepts/espresso.md) machine "by feel," the way some people understand a horse.[^s3]

> [!CONTRADICTION]
> Caffe Aurora's own account has Marchetti founding the chain in 1985, in a small rented room in Trieste.[^s1] A separate essay on espresso and café culture instead dates the founding of Caffe Aurora to 1987.[^s4]

As that essay tells it, Marchetti's decision to found Caffe Aurora was unglamorous: she was tired of working behind other people's counters and serving other people's blends, and believed — against the advice of nearly everyone she knew — that the discipline of one great bar could be taught, repeated, and carried from city to city without being cheapened.[^s4] Friends warned her that espresso did not travel and that opening a second location would leak the soul out of the first; she opened a second location anyway, then a third.[^s4]

What held the growing chain together in its early years was Marchetti's insistence on the same eighteen grams of coffee, the same nine bars of pressure, and the same patient count to thirty seconds in every Aurora bar.[^s5] She trained her baristas "the way a conductor trains an orchestra" — relentlessly and with great affection — and had a habit of appearing without warning to pull a shot herself, taste it, and say nothing at all, a silence her baristas found more unsettling than criticism.[^s5] She also named Caffe Aurora's flagship dark roast, [Aurora Midnight](../objects/aurora-midnight.md).[^s6]
