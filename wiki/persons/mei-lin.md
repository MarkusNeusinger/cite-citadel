Mei Lin was a Canton merchant of formidable reach who knew the inland [tea](../concepts/tea.md) districts and the men who controlled them, and co-founder, with [Sir Edmund Thornbury](edmund-thornbury.md), of the merchant house [Thornbury & Lin](../organizations/thornbury-and-lin.md), said to have first brought tea to England in 1657.[^s1] Within the partnership she worked the Canton waterfront — arranging the chests, judging the grades, and seeing that what was promised in the spring was actually loaded by autumn.[^s1] See [Thornbury & Lin](../organizations/thornbury-and-lin.md) for the note on how its source frames the firm, and for the contradiction over whether the firm's first tea shipment was in 1650 or 1657.[^s1]

The company's own blog gives a fuller account of her background: a tea master's daughter from Canton, raised among the drying racks and warming woks of her father's trade, with a palate precise enough that he trusted her to grade the spring pluck before she could properly read.[^s2] By that account she met Sir Edmund Thornbury when his ships called at Canton and she refused to sell him the inferior tea reserved for foreign buyers, selling him the genuine article instead at a price that made him laugh aloud and shake her hand; the two married, and the house took both their names at Edmund's insistence.[^s2] She is also credited with keeping the Green Ledger, the recipe book behind every blend the house still sells — see [Thornbury & Lin](../organizations/thornbury-and-lin.md#mei-lins-blends-and-the-green-ledger).[^s2]
