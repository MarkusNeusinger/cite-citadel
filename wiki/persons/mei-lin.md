Mei Lin was a Canton merchant of formidable reach who knew the inland tea districts and the men who controlled them.[^s1] In 1657 Mei Lin joined the English trader [Edmund Thornbury](edmund-thornbury.md) to found the merchant house of [Thornbury & Lin](../organizations/thornbury-and-lin.md), which went on to bring the first tea to England.[^s1] Mei Lin worked the Canton waterfront side of the partnership, arranging the chests, judging the grades, and seeing that what was promised in the spring was actually loaded by autumn.[^s1]

By the house's own account, Mei Lin was a tea master's daughter from Canton, raised among drying racks and warming woks, with a palate precise enough that her father trusted her to grade the spring pluck before she could properly read.[^s2] That account holds that she married Edmund Thornbury after refusing to sell him the low grade kept for foreign buyers and selling him the genuine article instead.[^s2] She later recorded her blend recipes, and the mood each was meant to suit, in a book the family calls the Green Ledger, and is said to have favored [Canton Mist](../objects/canton-mist.md), the house's lightly fired green tea, herself.[^s3]
